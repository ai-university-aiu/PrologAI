Chapter 319: Wave 101 - legend_snake (WP-359, Layer 334)

legend_snake solves ARC-AGI-2 task 195c6913. The task gives
three training pairs and one test case. The grids range from
20x20 to 30x30, and the output always keeps the input size.
Every input shows two large regions of two dominant colors
meeting along a jagged diagonal boundary, with stray cells of
each color scattered inside the other region. Near the top-left
corner a legend strip of two-by-two blocks, separated by single
background columns, spells out a color sequence - 2, 2, 8 in
the first pair, 4, 1 in the second, 2, 2, 3 in the third, and
1, 2, 8, 3 in the test. Elsewhere a lone two-by-two block of
one extra color - 4, 9, 8, and 6 across the four grids - names
the turn color. Single marker cells of the legend's first color
sit on the left edge inside one of the regions. In the output
every marker has fired a snake path eastward through its own
region, painted with the legend pattern repeating cyclically;
every time the path ran into the other region, the blocking
cell took the turn color and the snake pivoted north, then east
again at the next block, climbing the boundary like a
staircase; and the legend strip and the lone turn block have
been erased to the region color around them.

Rule: read the two region colors as the two most frequent
colors; collect every two-by-two block of a non-region color,
taking the blocks that share a top row as the legend pattern
and the lone leftover as the turn color; erase them; and walk
each left-edge marker eastward through its own region, painting
the pattern cyclically with the marker as position zero,
marking every blocking cell with the turn color, and pivoting
east to north or north to east without spending a pattern
position.

Algorithm:

The transform first calls ls_region_colors_, which flattens the
grid, counts the palette with clumped, and keeps the two most
frequent colors as the regions A and B. ls_blocks_ enumerates
every cell by row and column index and keeps each cell whose
color is neither A nor B and whose right, lower, and diagonal
neighbors repeat that color - the two-by-two blocks of the
scene. ls_legend_ picks the top row shared by at least two
blocks: those blocks, sorted by column, spell the repeating
pattern left to right, and exactly one block must remain, whose
color is the turn color. ls_markers_ collects every left-edge
row whose first cell carries the pattern's head color and whose
second cell differs from it, reading each marker's region color
from that neighboring cell. ls_erase_ then paints the four
cells of the turn block and of every legend block with the
region color that ls_fill_color_ probes from the block's four
orthogonal neighbors, reusing hb_paint_. Finally ls_path_
launches each marker's snake at pattern position one heading
east, and ls_walk_ with ls_step_ does the walking: an off-grid
next cell ends the path silently; a next cell of the marker's
region color takes the pattern color at position mod length and
advances the position; any other cell is a block - it takes the
turn color, spends no pattern position, and ls_turn_ pivots the
heading from east to north or from north to east, resuming from
the last path cell only if the pivoted neighbor holds the
region color, otherwise the path ends after the single mark.
Region membership is always read from the untouched input grid
while colors accumulate on the erased working copy, so painted
pattern cells can never block a later snake. The induce clause
dispatches early, directly after corner_glyph, requiring equal
first-pair dimensions, a legend strip with a lone turn block,
and at least one edge marker, then verifying every training
pair.

The lesson of the wave is that the turn bookkeeping carries the
whole task. The turn mark spends no pattern position, so the
cyclic pattern flows unbroken around every staircase corner -
verified position by position across all six snakes of the four
grids - and the second training pair's lower snake, whose pivot
direction is blocked immediately, proves that a blocked pivot
ends the path quietly after exactly one mark. The legend strip
is the program and the grid is the machine: reading the strip
left to right as a cyclic program executed by edge-launched
walkers turns a daunting scene into thirty lines of walking
code.

Key predicates:
