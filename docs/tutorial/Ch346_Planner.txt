Chapter 346: Hierarchical Planning - The planner Pack (ht_*, Layer 361)

Big goals are not achieved in one step. They are broken into parts, the
parts into smaller parts, until every piece is something you can actually
do. Planners call this a hierarchical task network (HTN): compound tasks
decompose through methods into subtasks, and decomposition bottoms out in
primitive actions. The agency pack runs the Observe-Reason-Act loop; this
pack gives that loop its long-horizon spine.

A domain declares primitives and methods:

    ?- ht_domain(
         [prim(walk(X, Y), [at(me, X), short(X, Y)],
               [at(me, Y)], [at(me, X)]),
          prim(call_taxi(X), [at(me, X)], [taxi_at(X)], []),
          prim(ride(X, Y), [at(me, X), taxi_at(X)],
               [at(me, Y)], [at(me, X), taxi_at(X)]),
          prim(pay, [has_cash], [paid], [has_cash])],
         [meth(go_by_foot, travel(X, Y), [short(X, Y)], [walk(X, Y)]),
          meth(go_by_taxi, travel(X, Y), [has_cash],
               [call_taxi(X), ride(X, Y), pay]),
          meth(two_leg, trip(X, Y, Z), [], [travel(X, Y), travel(Y, Z)])],
         Domain).

Two things to notice. Methods are tried in declaration order, so putting
go_by_foot first says "prefer walking". And a method's subtasks may
themselves be compound - trip decomposes into two travel tasks, each of
which decomposes again.

PLANNING

    ?- ht_plan(Domain, [at(me, home), short(home, park), has_cash],
               [travel(home, park)], 10, Plan).
    Plan = [walk(home, park)].

The park is close, so the preferred method wins. Remove the short-distance
fact and Prolog's own backtracking falls through to the taxi method:

    Plan = [call_taxi(home), ride(home, park), pay].

A nested trip mixes both: walk the short first leg, taxi the long second
leg. The depth bound counts decompositions, so even a method that
decomposes a task into itself forever terminates cleanly with failure
instead of hanging.

THE GLASS-BOX PLAN TREE

Where most planners give you a flat list of actions, ht_task_tree tells
you WHY each action is there:

    ?- ht_task_tree(Domain, State, travel(home, park), 10, Tree).
    Tree = tree(travel(home, park), go_by_foot,
                [primitive(walk(home, park))]).

Every compound task in the tree names the method that was chosen for it.
This is explainable planning - the same glass-box discipline as the named
rules of the ARC-AGI climb.

EXECUTION, MONITORING, REPLANNING

ht_execute runs a plan against a state. ht_monitor walks a plan and
reports either ok or exactly where it breaks:

    ?- ht_monitor(Domain, [at(me, home)],
                  [call_taxi(home), ride(home, park), pay], Status).
    Status = fails_at(3, pay).

The cash is gone, so step three can no longer run. Armed with that,
ht_replan plans again from the situation as it now stands - and if the
park has meanwhile become walkable, walking saves the day.

All 18 acceptance tests pass: preference, fall-back, nesting, termination,
trees, execution, monitoring, and recovery.
