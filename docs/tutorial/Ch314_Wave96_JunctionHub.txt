Chapter 314: Wave 96 - junction_hub (WP-354, Layer 329)

junction_hub solves ARC-AGI-2 task 800d221b. The task gives
three training pairs and one test case. The grids are 16x16,
10x10, and 20x20 in training and 30x30 in the test, and the
output always keeps the input size. Every scene draws a wire
network on a uniform background using exactly three foreground
colors: one wire color forming the network and two content
colors mixed together inside the small blocks that the wires
join. In the first training pair the wires are 8s joining
blocks of mixed 3s and 4s on a 9 background; in the second the
wires are 5s joining blocks of 2s and 1s on a 0 background; in
the third the wires are 7s joining blocks of 6s and 5s on an 8
background; and in the test the wires are 4s joining blocks of
3s and 2s on a 6 background. The output repaints every wire
cell with the majority content color of the block its branch of
the network reaches, except at the crossing point: there a
three-by-three ring keeps the wire color and the single cell at
its center takes the color of the winning side.

Rule: find the hub as the only cell whose full three-by-three
neighborhood holds the wire color, keep its eight ring cells
wire colored, split the remaining wire into four-connected
branches, repaint each branch with the majority content color
of the blocks it touches, and paint the hub with the majority
color of the branch cells touching the ring.

Algorithm:

The transform first takes the most common color as the
background with arc2_bg_color_ and requires exactly four
distinct colors in the grid. jh_centers_ and jh_solid_ then
census, for each foreground color, the cells whose full
three-by-three window holds that color: the wire color is the
only one owning exactly one such cell - the content colors own
none, because their blocks are small and mixed, or many,
because their solid regions are large - and that cell is the
hub. The eight cells around the hub form the ring; the hub and
ring leave the wire set and the ring keeps the wire color in
the output. jh_cells_ lists the content cells and the wire
cells keyed by position, and jh_parts_ with jh_flood_ splits
each set into four-connected components over a position assoc -
the content cells into mixed-color blocks and the ring-free
wire cells into branches. jh_branch_color_ repaints every
branch: it collects the blocks holding a cell four-adjacent to
the branch with jh_meets_ and takes the majority color over all
cells of those blocks with jh_majority_, which run-length
encodes the sorted colors through the Wave 95 helper scc_runs_.
Every branch cell four-adjacent to a ring cell then votes its
branch color once, and the hub takes the majority vote. Finally
jh_paint_branch_ writes every branch through scc_paint_ and
arc2_set_cell_ paints the hub. The induce clause dispatches
early, directly after short_circuit, requiring equal first-pair
dimensions and exactly four distinct colors in the first input,
then verifying every training pair.

The lesson of the wave is that the hub verdict is a cell-level
election, not a branch-level one. In the third training pair
the losing color owns three branches against two, yet the
winning color takes the hub because its two branches each wrap
onto the ring at two separate places and outvote the others
four cells to three - a branch touching the ring twice carries
double weight. A second lesson extends Wave 95: the four colors
permute freely across pairs, and this time no cell-count
ranking can name the wire, so the rule recognizes it by a
purely structural signature - the unique solid three-by-three
window - derived fresh from every grid. A third lesson is that
adjacency arity matters: the arms of the network run side by
side one background cell apart, so the branches separate only
under four-way adjacency, and the eight-way component helpers
of the earlier waves would fuse them diagonally.

Key predicates:
  jh_centers_/5        - solid three-by-three centers of a color
  jh_solid_/4          - test one full three-by-three window
  jh_cells_/3          - list cells of given colors by position
  jh_step_/4           - one four-connected neighbour of a cell
  jh_parts_/4          - four-connected components of a cell set
  jh_flood_/4          - four-connected flood fill over an assoc
  jh_branch_color_/6   - majority block color touching a branch
  jh_meets_/2          - does an adjacency set enter a block
  jh_majority_/2       - most frequent value of a list
  jh_paint_branch_/3   - paint one branch onto the grid
  arc2_transform(junction_hub, +Grid, -Out)
  arc2_induce_rule(+Pairs, junction_hub)
