Chapter 147: Objmorph Pack (om_*, Layer 165)

The objmorph pack (om_* prefix, Layer 165) provides 14 predicates for binary
morphological operations on obj(Color, Cells) terms. Where the morph pack (mo_*)
operates on 2D grids, the objmorph pack operates directly on obj terms without
requiring a grid. It implements: neighborhood generation, boundary and interior
extraction under 4-connectivity and 8-connectivity, single-step and iterated
dilation, single-step and iterated erosion, and compound opening and closing.

147.1 Loading

:- use_module(pack(objmorph)).

147.2 Neighborhood Generators

objmorph_neighbors4(+Cell, -Neighbors) returns the 4 orthogonally adjacent cells.

    ?- objmorph_neighbors4(r(2,3), N).
    N = [r(1,3), r(3,3), r(2,2), r(2,4)].

objmorph_neighbors8(+Cell, -Neighbors) returns all 8 neighbors including diagonals.

    ?- objmorph_neighbors8(r(0,0), N).
    N = [r(-1,-1),r(-1,0),r(-1,1),r(0,-1),r(0,1),r(1,-1),r(1,0),r(1,1)].

Both predicates always return the fixed number of cells (4 or 8) regardless
of grid boundaries; coordinates may be negative.

147.3 Boundary and Interior (4-Connectivity)

objmorph_boundary4(+Obj, -Boundary): a cell is a boundary4 cell if at least one of
its 4 neighbors is outside the cell set.

    ?- objmorph_boundary4(obj(red,[r(0,0),r(0,1),r(1,0),r(1,1)]), R).
    R = obj(red,[r(0,0),r(0,1),r(1,0),r(1,1)]).  % all 4 boundary for 2x2

objmorph_interior4(+Obj, -Interior): a cell is interior4 only if all 4 neighbors
are inside the cell set. A 3x3 square has exactly one interior cell.

    ?- objmorph_interior4(obj(b,[r(0,0),r(0,1),r(0,2),r(1,0),r(1,1),r(1,2),
                          r(2,0),r(2,1),r(2,2)]), I).
    I = obj(b,[r(1,1)]).

147.4 Boundary and Interior (8-Connectivity)

objmorph_boundary8 and objmorph_interior8 work like their 4-connected counterparts but
require all 8 neighbors to be inside for a cell to be interior8. This is
stricter: a 5x5 square has 9 interior8 cells (the inner 3x3) but 8 interior4
cells in a 3x3 is just the center.

    ?- square_cells(5, C), objmorph_interior8(obj(b,C), I), I = obj(b,IL), length(IL, N).
    N = 9.

147.5 Dilation

objmorph_dilate4(+Obj, -Result): expand the cell set by one 4-connected step. Each
original cell contributes its 4 neighbors; the original cells are kept.

    ?- objmorph_dilate4(obj(red,[r(0,0)]), R).
    R = obj(red,[r(-1,0),r(0,-1),r(0,0),r(0,1),r(1,0)]).

objmorph_dilate8(+Obj, -Result): expand by one 8-connected step. A single cell
expands to a 3x3 block of 9 cells.

    ?- objmorph_dilate8(obj(a,[r(5,5)]), obj(a,D)), length(D, N).
    N = 9.

147.6 Erosion

objmorph_erode4(+Obj, -Result): shrink by one 4-connected step; delegates to
objmorph_interior4. Removes all boundary4 cells.

objmorph_erode8(+Obj, -Result): shrink by one 8-connected step; delegates to
objmorph_interior8. Stricter — more cells are removed.

    ?- square_cells(4, C), objmorph_erode4(obj(b,C), E), E = obj(b,EL), length(EL, N).
    N = 4.  % inner 2x2 survives

147.7 Iterated Operations

objmorph_dilate4_n(+Obj, +N, -Result) and objmorph_erode4_n(+Obj, +N, -Result) apply
their respective operations N times. N=0 is the identity.

    ?- objmorph_dilate4_n(obj(x,[r(0,0)]), 2, obj(x,D)), length(D, N).
    N = 13.  % diamond of Manhattan radius 2

    ?- square_cells(5, C), objmorph_erode4_n(obj(b,C), 2, E).
    E = obj(b,[r(2,2)]).  % center only after two erosions

147.8 Opening and Closing

objmorph_open4(+Obj, -Result): erosion followed by dilation. Removes thin
protrusions while preserving bulk shapes.

    ?- square_cells(3, C), objmorph_open4(obj(b,C), obj(b,O)), sort(O, S).
    S = [r(0,1),r(1,0),r(1,1),r(1,2),r(2,1)].  % plus shape

objmorph_close4(+Obj, -Result): dilation followed by erosion. Fills small gaps
while preserving bulk shapes.

    ?- objmorph_close4(obj(red,[r(0,0)]), R).
    R = obj(red,[r(0,0)]).  % single cell is closed back to itself

147.9 Predicate Reference

Neighborhood:
  objmorph_neighbors4(+Cell, -Neighbors)
  objmorph_neighbors8(+Cell, -Neighbors)

Boundary and Interior:
  objmorph_boundary4(+Obj, -Boundary)
  objmorph_interior4(+Obj, -Interior)
  objmorph_boundary8(+Obj, -Boundary)
  objmorph_interior8(+Obj, -Interior)

Single-Step Morphology:
  objmorph_dilate4(+Obj, -Result)
  objmorph_erode4(+Obj, -Result)
  objmorph_dilate8(+Obj, -Result)
  objmorph_erode8(+Obj, -Result)

Iterated Morphology:
  objmorph_dilate4_n(+Obj, +N, -Result)
  objmorph_erode4_n(+Obj, +N, -Result)

Compound Operations:
  objmorph_open4(+Obj, -Result)
  objmorph_close4(+Obj, -Result)
