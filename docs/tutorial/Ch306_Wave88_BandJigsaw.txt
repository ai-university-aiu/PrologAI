Chapter 306: Wave 88 - band_jigsaw (WP-346, Layer 321)

band_jigsaw solves ARC-AGI-2 task 16b78196. The task gives two
training pairs and one test case, all on 30x30 grids, with every
output the same size as its input. Each grid holds one wide band
component whose bounding box spans the full grid width - or, in the
test case, the full grid height - with ragged, notched edges, plus
three to five small single-color shapes scattered around it. The
output keeps the band exactly where it is, erases the shapes from
their original positions, and restacks every shape flush against
the band so the pieces interlock like a jigsaw puzzle.

Rule: every shape is translated - never rotated, flipped, or
recolored - so that the shapes on each side of the band exactly
tile the region between the band's ragged edge profile and a
perfectly straight outer edge, plugging the band's notches on the
way; the side split, the intervals, and every placement are found
by search.

Algorithm:

The majority color is the background, and the foreground cells
group into 8-connected components with the reused helpers
tw_background_ and tw_components_ from the twin_lift pack. bj_span_
selects the unique component whose bounding box spans a full grid
dimension as the band, and bj_canon_dims_ with bj_canon_cells_
transposes a vertical band into the canonical horizontal frame.
bj_profile_ reads the band's bottom profile as the maximum band row
per column and its top profile as the minimum, and bj_flipnum_
reflects the top profile vertically so both sides solve as a
bottom-side chain. bj_partition_ assigns each shape to a side by
backtracking search, bj_norm_ normalizes each shape to a sorted
cell list at its bounding-box corner, and bj_flipnorm_ does the
same after a vertical flip for the far side. bj_chain_ picks a
column interval at least as wide as the widest assigned shape and
forces the straight outer edge row from the exact-area equation -
interval width times outer row minus profile sum equals total shape
area - then bj_tile_ exact-tiles the region by anchored exact
cover: the row-major first uncovered cell must host some unused
shape's row-major first cell. bj_unflip_ and bj_uncanon_cell_ map
the placements back, and the reused tt_render_ paints band plus
placements onto a fresh background canvas. The induce clause
dispatches early, directly after twin_toggle, requiring preserved
dimensions, 20 to 500 foreground cells, exactly one full-span
component, and two to eight movable shapes of at most 40 cells
before verifying every training pair.

The lesson: the human-steps description splits the shapes into wide
ones above the band and narrow ones below, but the second training
pair stacks all three shapes below the band, so the side split must
be searched rather than assumed. Hand-tracing all six grids before
coding exposed the real invariant - a straight outer edge - and
that edge turns out to be a computed quantity, not a searched one:
given an interval, arithmetic forces it, collapsing the packing
search to a handful of exact-cover calls. The test grid rotates the
whole scene ninety degrees, with a vertical band and chains on its
left and right, so the orientation-general canonicalization by
transposition plus vertical flip was mandatory, exactly as the
Wave 85-87 surveys predicted. The rule passed both training pairs
and the test case on the first prototype run and the full
regression on the first compile.
