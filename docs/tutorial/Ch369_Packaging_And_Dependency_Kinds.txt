Chapter 369 — Packaging and Dependency Kinds: packaging (Wave 10 Stage 7)

This chapter closes the Requirements Ledger's Theme G. It matters most when a system is
cut into very many small packs - as the one-pack-per-construct experiment was - because
then every reference between two pieces becomes a full inter-pack import, and PrologAI
had only ONE kind of import with no way to say what it was for.

Plain-language idea. Not all dependencies are the same. Some are needed only to build a
data record once (a mint-time need); others are needed to actually run behaviour. Some
consumers want a whole family of packs at once; others want to load just the data side
of a pack without pulling in all its running machinery. The packaging pack adds four
plain notions for all of this.

One - dependency kinds. You declare a dependency as structure_only (needed only to mint
a record) or runtime (needed to run behaviour). packaging_runtime_dependencies then
lists only the runtime ones - the edges that actually matter for the layer graph - so
mint-time-only edges stop inflating it.

Two - loadable faces. A pack has two faces: a structure face and a runtime face. A
structure_only dependency needs only the target's structure face; a runtime dependency
needs its runtime face. packaging_face_dependencies tells you exactly what loading one
face pulls in, so you can validate a record without dragging in the runtime substrate.

Three - a facade (a bundle). packaging_declare_facade names a group of packs (and a group
may contain other groups). packaging_expand turns a group name into the concrete list of
packs, following nested groups and never looping. A consumer names the bundle instead of
listing every small pack by hand.

Four - a cross-pack record registry. packaging_register_record files a content-addressed
record under its id and its owning pack; packaging_record and packaging_record_owner look
it up by id. Ids are looked up in one place instead of being threaded through
hand-written accessor predicates.

Public operations: packaging_declare_dependency/3, packaging_dependency/3,
packaging_runtime_dependencies/2, packaging_structure_only_dependencies/2,
packaging_required_face/2, packaging_face_dependencies/3, packaging_declare_facade/2,
packaging_facade/2, packaging_expand/2, packaging_register_record/3, packaging_record/2,
and packaging_record_owner/2.
