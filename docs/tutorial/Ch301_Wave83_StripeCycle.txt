Chapter 301: Wave 83 - stripe_cycle (WP-341, Layer 316)

stripe_cycle solves ARC-AGI-2 task 221dfab4. The task gives two
training pairs on 29x22 grids and one test case on a 25x25 grid. One
edge row of each input carries a horizontal bar of color 4: the
bottom row in both training inputs but the top row in the test
input. The bar's columns are the stripe columns. The rest of the
grid scatters one-color shapes over a majority background -
background 1 with color-8 shapes in the first training pair,
background 8 with color-2 shapes in the second, and background 8
with color-6 shapes in the test.

Rule: repaint every row by a six-row palette keyed to its cyclic
distance D from the marker row; D of 0 or 2 paints the stripe
columns with the marker color 4, odd D paints them with the
background color, and D of 4 paints the stripe columns with the
overlay color 3 while also overlaying 3 on every non-background
cell of that row outside the stripes.

Algorithm:

The background is the most frequent color in the grid - counted, not
assumed, because the grids use backgrounds 1 and 8; the helper
tw_background_ from the twin_lift pack is reused unchanged. The
marker row is whichever of the top row and the bottom row holds
cells of color 4, and the columns of those cells are the stripe
columns. Every row is then rebuilt under the palette entry for its
cyclic distance D, the absolute row difference from the marker row
modulo six. At D of 0 or 2 the stripe columns take the marker color
4 and every other cell keeps its input value. At odd D the stripe
columns take the background color and every other cell keeps its
input value. At D of 4 the stripe columns take the overlay color 3,
and every non-background cell of the row outside the stripes is
also overlaid with 3, while background cells outside the stripes
keep their value. Nothing else in the grid moves. The induce clause
dispatches early, directly after twin_lift, requiring preserved
dimensions, a color-4 marker bar of at least two cells in an edge
row, and no color-4 cell outside the marker row before verifying
every training pair.

The lesson: the palette anchor was the whole task. Both training
grids put the marker bar in the bottom row at row 28, and 28 mod 6
equals 4, so a palette counted from row zero reproduces both
training outputs perfectly and still fails the hidden test - the
test grid moves the bar to the top row, where a row-zero count
would paint the marker bar itself green. Anchoring the cycle at the
marker row survives both placements. When two readings tie on the
training pairs, the test input often breaks the tie; reading every
visible grid before coding turned a trap into a first-compile pass.

Key predicates:
  st_marker_/3         - find the color-4 edge marker bar
  st_row_/5            - rebuild one output row under the palette
  st_cell_/6           - palette value of one cell
  arc2_transform(stripe_cycle, +Grid, -Out)
  arc2_induce_rule(+Pairs, stripe_cycle)
