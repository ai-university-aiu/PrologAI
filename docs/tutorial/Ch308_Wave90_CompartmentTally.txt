Chapter 308: Wave 90 - compartment_tally (WP-348, Layer 323)

compartment_tally solves ARC-AGI-2 task 89565ca0. The task gives
three training pairs and one test case, with inputs from 22x20 up
to 30x28 and tiny census outputs from 3x4 up to 5x6. Each input
holds one scattered noise color plus three to five box colors on a
majority background. Every box color draws exactly one rectangular
outline, possibly subdivided by internal partition lines - full or
partial - into table-like compartments, one to six per box. The
boxes nest inside each other, overlap, and cross each other's
lines, and the scattered noise cells land everywhere, including on
top of box-line cells. The output is a census: one row per box,
sorted by compartment count ascending, each row painting the box
color once per compartment and padding with the noise color out to
the maximum count.

Rule: the census digits are structural - each box's number is the
count of compartments its partition lines enclose, found by flood
fill with guarded single-cell wall repair, and the boxes are listed
from fewest to most compartments with ties broken by the leftmost
bounding-box column.

Algorithm:

The majority color is the background with the reused helper
tw_background_, and ww_groups_ collects one cell set per remaining
color. ct_comps_ counts the 4-connected fragments of every color's
cell set, and the color with the most fragments is the scattered
noise color, because noise cells are mostly isolated singletons
while box outlines cohere into a few large fragments. For each
remaining color, ct_box_ frames the box with the reused ww_ends_
bounding box and collects every open cell strictly inside it. The
wall test ct_wall_ marks a cell as blocking when it carries the box
color itself, or - the guarded repair - when it is non-background
and both its vertical neighbors or both its horizontal neighbors
carry the box color, which restores partition cells that noise or
another box's crossing line overwrote while keeping genuine
single-row and single-column compartments open. ct_comps_ then
counts the 4-connected open regions with the ct_grow_ flood fill,
and that region count is the box's compartment count. Nested and
crossing boxes need no special handling, because their cells are
not walls for the box under analysis and closed outlines passing
through do not split compartments. Finally the boxes sort by
compartment count with msort - the b(N, C1, R1, V) term order gives
the leftmost-column tie-break for free - and ct_row_ emits one
census row per box, the box color repeated once per compartment,
padded with the noise color to the maximum count. The induce clause
dispatches early, directly after wire_wrap, requiring the first
pair's output strictly smaller than its input in both dimensions,
census bounds of two to nine, and a distinct foreground color count
equal to the output height plus one, then verifies every training
pair.

The lesson of the wave is the guarded gap repair. A naive flood
fill with walls of the box color only leaks through every partition
cell that noise or a crossing box overwrote, undercounting the
compartments. Repairing every flanked cell fixes the leaks but
walls off genuine single-row and single-column compartments, whose
interior background cells are also flanked by two parallel lines.
Guarding the repair to non-background cells fixes both failure
modes at once: real gaps are always noise or crossing cells, never
background.

Key predicates:

  ct_wall_/5           - guarded wall test for the flood fill
  ct_wall_x_/6         - wall classification of one cell
  ct_comps_/2          - 4-connected component count of a cell set
  ct_comps_s_/2        - component count of an ordered cell set
  ct_grow_/3           - absorb one component's cells
  ct_row_/5            - one census row for one box
