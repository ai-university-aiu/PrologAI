Chapter 329: Wave 111 - fractal_stamp (WP-369, Layer 344)

fractal_stamp solves ARC-AGI-2 task a32d8b75. The task gives
three training pairs on 20x30 grids and one test case on a 30x30
grid. Every input carries one or two LEGEND PANELS beside
full-height divider columns of color 6 - each training grid has a
single panel on the left, and the test grid has one panel on each
side. The output drops the panels and the dividers entirely, so
only the PLAYING FIELD between them survives: training outputs
are 20x24 and the test output is 30x20.

Each panel is a four-part stamping program. Above the panel's
first all-6 separator row lies the glyph zone, holding a STAMP
glyph drawn in exactly two colors and a larger LAYOUT glyph drawn
in exactly one color. Between the two separator rows lies the
ANCHOR BOX, holding a single 4 whose corner position names the
corner of the field where the painting is anchored flush. Below
the second separator lies the ROTATION BOX, holding a two-cell
7-domino needle: vertical in the upper half commands no rotation,
vertical in the lower half a half turn, horizontal in the right
half a quarter turn clockwise, horizontal in the left half three
quarter turns.

Rule: find every full-height 6-column and give each one a panel
toward the nearer grid edge, keeping the middle as the field; per
panel, split the glyph zone into 8-connected components, take the
two-color component as the stamp (cut at its bounding box, its
two colors exchanged) and the one-color component as the layout
(a boolean mask over its bounding box); decode the anchor corner
and the needle rotation; then rotate the mask, scale it up by the
stamp's height and width, place the region flush at the anchored
field corner, and paint a full stamp copy at every set mask cell
while every clear cell keeps the field pixels. Several panels
fold over the shared field one after another.

The delicate part is that the anchor and the rotation form a
TWO-PART command that only makes sense read jointly. Across the
five panels of the task the pairs are top-left with no turn,
top-right with a quarter turn, bottom-right with a half turn,
bottom-right with no turn, and top-left with a half turn - the
same corner appears with two different rotations and the same
rotation with two different corners, so any single-marker theory
collapses, yet the pair predicts all five placements exactly.

Two details matter. Box interiors are obtained by trimming every
all-6 column from the box rows rather than assuming fixed border
widths - the divider-adjacent border column otherwise shifts the
interior center and misreads a vertical needle as horizontal,
the one bug the Python prototype caught before any Prolog was
written. And the painted stamp is the legend glyph's NEGATIVE -
its two colors exchanged - echoing Wave 110, where reflected
snakes showed their undersides. The induce clause dispatches
early, directly after snake_dock, requiring equal heights, a
strictly narrower output, and a full-height 6-column on the
first pair, then verifying every training pair exactly. The
integrated rule passed the single-task test and the full
regression on the first compile.
