Chapter 365 — Temporal Enactment: the tick_scheduler (Wave 10 Stage 3)

This chapter closes the Requirements Ledger's highest-priority wall - Theme A,
temporal and scheduling (the HIPPO-2 and CEREBELLUM-1 sightings). Before it,
PrologAI could describe a timed process but could not ENACT one: consolidation ran
the instant a memory was written, and the cerebellum - whose defining job is timing -
had to record a tick as "seconds" because there was no way to schedule and fire a
future reactivation.

Plain-language idea. A mind does some things not now but LATER - it consolidates a
memory hours after forming it, it expects a consequence a few steps after an action.
"Later" here is counted in ordinal ticks: a plain, dimensionless count of steps, not
seconds on a wall clock. The tick_scheduler is a clock-and-schedule kept in the shared
Lattice store: a current tick, and a list of reactivations each due at some future
tick. You advance the clock; whatever has come due fires, in due-tick order, and is
handed back (or run) so it can be enacted.

The tick_scheduler pack (WP-432, layer 0). Public operations:

 • tick_scheduler_open(Address, Nexus) - open a Lattice nexus and start its clock at
   zero with an empty schedule.
 • tick_scheduler_now(Nexus, Tick) - read the current ordinal tick.
 • tick_scheduler_schedule_after(Nexus, Delay, Label, Payload) - schedule a
   reactivation Delay ticks into the future.
 • tick_scheduler_schedule_at(Nexus, DueTick, Label, Payload) - schedule at an
   absolute future tick.
 • tick_scheduler_schedule_after_unit(Nexus, Delay, Unit, Label, Payload) - the same,
   but the Unit must be ordinal; a wall-clock unit (seconds) is refused.
 • tick_scheduler_pending(Nexus, List) - the still-pending reactivations, in order.
 • tick_scheduler_tick(Nexus, Fired) - advance one tick.
 • tick_scheduler_advance(Nexus, Delta, Fired) - advance Delta ticks; return and
   remove whatever became due.
 • tick_scheduler_advance_enact(Nexus, Delta, Goal, Fired) - advance, then run Goal on
   each fired reactivation, in due-tick order.

Why ordinal ticks and not seconds. Time is counted in ticks - the Causalontology 3.0.0
ordinal unit, a dimensionless integer. The scheduler asks the Causalontology core
(causal_core_dimension) whether a unit is ordinal, and refuses a wall-clock unit with a
clear category error. That is the exact closure of CEREBELLUM-1: no tick is forced into
a "seconds" distortion.

How it closes HIPPO-2. A deferred consolidation is scheduled a number of ticks out and
enacted only when the ordinal clock reaches its due tick - a real scheduler, where
before there was none, so the region no longer has to consolidate synchronously.
