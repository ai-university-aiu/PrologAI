Chapter 318: Wave 100 - corner_glyph (WP-358, Layer 333)

corner_glyph solves ARC-AGI-2 task 4c416de3 - on the one
hundredth wave of the climb. The task gives three training
pairs and one test case. The grids range from 19x22 to 23x23,
and the output always keeps the input size. Every input holds
boxes outlined in the zero border color on a solid background -
1, 8, 2, and 3 across the four grids - some clipped by the grid
edge. One box corner already carries a full multi-cell
decoration, the template, and the template shape differs in
every grid: a two-by-two outward block plus two inward diagonal
cells in the first pair, a six-cell zigzag band in the second,
a four-cell corner cross in the third, and a six-cell hook in
the test. Other corners carry just a single colored cell - a
glyph - sitting at one specific position of the shape: its most
inward cell on the corner's own diagonal. The output completes
every glyph into the full decoration, painted in the glyph's
color and mirrored into that corner's orientation, while
unmarked corners stay bare.

Rule: detect every box corner with its outward orientation by a
tolerant L-junction test; learn the template shape from the one
corner holding at least three same-colored cells nearby, each
cell stored as an offset in the corner's own frame; take the
shape's most inward diagonal offset as the marker position; and
stamp the complete shape, in the marker cell's color, at every
corner whose marker cell holds a non-background, non-zero
color.

Algorithm:

The transform first reads the background with arc2_bg_color_
and requires it to differ from zero. cg_corner_ then scans
every cell and every orientation frame (u, v): a corner cell is
never background, cg_arm_ validates the two border arms running
inward - their first four in-bounds cells must never show the
background, though decoration cells may overwrite border cells
anywhere on the arm, and at least two zero border cells must
survive per arm - while cg_not_zero_ confirms that no border
continues outward and the inward diagonal cell is interior.
cg_template_ enumerates the detected corners and collects every
non-background, non-zero cell within Chebyshev distance two in
frame coordinates (p = dr times u, q = dc times v); a color
reaching at least three cells is the template and its offsets
are the shape, so one canonical shape serves all four corner
orientations. cg_marker_ takes the shape's most inward offset
on its own diagonal - p equals q, p negative, p minimal - as
the marker position; the first pair's template reaches two
diagonal steps inward while the other grids reach one, so the
marker is read off the learned shape, never hard-coded. The
transform then reads each corner's marker cell and keeps every
corner whose marker holds a color that is neither background
nor zero, and cg_stamp_ folds over them, mapping every shape
offset through the corner's own frame and painting the marker
color via hb_paint_, which clips at the grid edges. Template
corners pass the marker test and repaint themselves unchanged,
so the stamp is idempotent. The induce clause dispatches early,
directly after hole_beacon, requiring equal first-pair
dimensions, a nonzero background, zero borders present, and a
template corner found, then verifying every training pair.

The lesson of the wave is that when decorations overwrite the
very structure that locates them - corner stamps painting over
their own box borders - the detector must be exactly as
tolerant as the decoration is destructive, and no more. The
first arm test demanded exact zeros at distances three and four
and lost two corners of the third pair's five-cell box, because
the template at one corner sits within arm reach of the
opposite corner. Forbidding only background on the arm while
counting two surviving zeros admits every true corner and,
checked against all thirty-one corners of the four grids, not
one impostor: every false candidate still fails on the zero
count or on the perpendicular arm.

  cg_corner_/6         - box corner with its outward frame
  cg_template_/4       - template color and shape in frame offsets
