Chapter 305: Wave 87 - twin_toggle (WP-345, Layer 320)

twin_toggle solves ARC-AGI-2 task c7f57c3e. The task gives two
training pairs and one test case, on grids of 18x18, 18x18, and
28x28, with every output the same size as its input. Each grid
holds several shapes on a uniform majority-color background. In the
first pair the shapes are small plants - a flower of 1s with a 2
center above a stem and a base - and the stem-and-base region is
drawn either in colors 2 and 3 or entirely in color 8. In the
second pair the shapes are X-bodies of 1s with a 2 center carrying
either a single 4 attached below or a single 6 attached above. The
test mixes 3-center and 6-center bodies with a 2-decoration above
or a 4-decoration below, and some shapes are exact scaled-up copies
of the small ones: scale 2 in training and scale 3 in the test.

Rule: every grid contains integer-scaled copies of exactly two
variant templates that share an identical body of same-colored
cells under a best-overlap alignment; the output redraws every
shape as the other variant at its own scale, positioned so the
shared body cells stay exactly where they were.

Algorithm:

The majority color is the background, and the foreground cells
group into 8-connected components with the reused helpers
tw_background_ and tw_components_ from the twin_lift pack.
tt_shape_ crops each component to its bounding box on a clean local
canvas holding only its own cells, and tt_reduce_ fully reduces the
crop by the largest integer scale whose square blocks are all
uniform - tt_blocky_ checks the blocks and tt_shrink_ samples them
- yielding a canonical template and a scale factor, so the scale-2
and scale-3 copies collapse onto the same canonical templates as
their small twins. The grid must present exactly two distinct
canonical templates. tt_align_ scores every translation offset of
the second template against the first by the count of overlapping
cells agreeing in color and keeps the maximum; those agreeing cells
are the shared body. tt_draw_ then redraws every shape as the OTHER
template, upscaled by the shape's own scale factor and positioned
through the alignment offset so the shared body stays fixed, and
tt_render_ paints the toggled cells onto a fresh background canvas
after a bounds check. The induce clause dispatches early, directly
after xray_frame, requiring preserved dimensions, a sparse
foreground of 8 to 400 cells, at least two components, and exactly
two canonical templates before verifying every training pair.

The lesson: the human-steps description frames this task as three
color roles - a base, a mid, and a pivot - with an adjacency test
choosing between a color swap and a color promotion. That reading
is hard to code and does not generalize: the first pair recolors a
stem and base, the second pair moves a marker from below one shape
to above another while changing 4 to 6, and the test does both at
once. Dumping all six grids and writing the complete rule in plain
English before coding revealed the far simpler twin-toggle
invariant, and one uniform mechanism - canonicalize, pair, align,
toggle - covers all three decoration styles with no per-color
reasoning at all. The rule passed both training pairs and the test
case on the first prototype run and the full regression on the
first compile.

Key predicates:
  tt_shape_/3          - canonical template, scale, and origin
  tt_reduce_/5         - maximal uniform block reduction
  tt_blocky_/4         - every SxS block is uniform
  tt_shrink_/5         - sample block colors into the template
  tt_align_/5          - best same-colored overlap offset
  tt_draw_/7           - one toggled twin cell
  tt_render_/5         - fresh canvas with the toggled shapes

  arc2_induce_rule(+Pairs, twin_toggle)
