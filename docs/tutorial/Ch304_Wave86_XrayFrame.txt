Chapter 304: Wave 86 - xray_frame (WP-344, Layer 319)

xray_frame solves ARC-AGI-2 task a6f40cea. The task gives three
training pairs and one test case, on inputs of 20x20, 30x30, 22x22,
and 24x24 with small cropped outputs of 4x7, 13x14, 7x8, and 8x10.
Each input holds one large rectangular frame of a single color
whose interior is uniformly filled - with the background color in
two grids and with a distinct fill color in the others - plus
several partial rectangle outlines drawn outside the frame. The
outlines visibly slide under the frame: their edges break off at
the frame boundary and, in one grid, re-emerge on the far side.
Two grids include outlines whose edges alternate two colors cell
by cell.

Rule: the output is the frame interior with every shape's hidden
cells restored, as if the frame were transparent - an x-ray of the
occluded region; each shape's bounding-box perimeter is completed
through the frame, single-color shapes restore their own color,
and two-color alternating shapes restore the color-swapped mirror
(vertical first, then horizontal) of the visible cell inside the
shape's own bounding box.

Algorithm:

The majority color is the background. Candidate frames are found by
xr_ring_: single-color 8-connected components (via the reused
helper tw_components_ from the twin_lift pack) whose cells form
exactly the perimeter of their bounding box, at least 3x3, with a
uniformly single-colored interior; xr_frame_ keeps the largest
candidate by bounding-box area, so the small perfect boxes
elsewhere in the test grid remain ordinary shapes. xr_shapes_
collects every non-background cell strictly outside the frame's
bounding box, groups the cells into 8-connected components, and
merges components sharing an identical color set - reuniting
outline halves that the frame split apart. xr_hidden_ walks each
shape's bounding-box perimeter and keeps the cells strictly inside
the frame interior, and xr_restore_ paints them: a single-color
shape paints its own color, while a two-color shape reflects the
hidden cell to the opposite edge of its own bounding box (vertical
mirror first, horizontal as fallback), reads the visible color
there with xr_visible_, and paints the swapped color with xr_swap_.
xr_out_cell_ assembles the interior crop with the restored cells
overlaid. The induce clause dispatches early, directly after
dial_rotor, requiring a strictly smaller output whose dimensions
equal the frame interior dimensions before verifying every
training pair.

The lesson: the two-color alternating outlines are a trap for every
positional-parity reading. Every visible cell of every two-color
shape in all four grids obeys a plain (row + column) checkerboard,
and the checkerboard correctly restores three of the four
alternating shapes - but the 9/8 shape in the third training pair
demands the exact anti-checkerboard in its hidden cells. Plain
alternation continuation, perimeter-path alternation,
border-skipping alternation, 180-degree rotation, and periodic
translation all fail on at least one shape. Only the color-swapped
mirror within the shape's own bounding box explains every hidden
cell of every shape, and writing out every hidden cell of every
training pair by hand before coding is what exposed both the trap
and its escape. The rule passed the single-task test and the full
regression on the first compile.

Key predicates:
  xr_frame_/6          - largest perfect ring with uniform interior
  xr_ring_/7           - one candidate single-color ring
  xr_shapes_/7         - outside shapes merged by color set
  xr_hidden_/9         - hidden perimeter cells inside the interior
  xr_restore_/13       - restored color via the swap-mirror rule
  xr_out_cell_/5       - interior crop cell with overlay
  arc2_transform(xray_frame, +Grid, -Out)
  arc2_induce_rule(+Pairs, xray_frame)
