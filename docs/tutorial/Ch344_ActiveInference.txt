Chapter 344: Active Inference - The actinf Pack (ai_*, Layer 359)

PrologAI has had the perception side of active inference since the
prediction pack: form predictions, compare them with reality, weight the
errors by precision, and update. This chapter adds the action side - the
part that decides what to do - and with it one of the most beautiful
results in the study of general intelligence: curiosity and goal-seeking
falling out of a single equation.

A generative model gm(States, Obs, A, B, C, D) says: which hidden states
the world can be in, which observations they produce (A), how actions move
the world between states (B), which outcomes the agent prefers (C), and
what it believes at the start (D). Every distribution is a plain list of
Name-Probability pairs, validated to sum to one.

Consider a T-maze. A reward sits in the left or right arm - the agent does
not know which. A cue at the bottom of the maze reveals the side. Three
actions: check the cue, go left, go right.

PERCEPTION IS BAYES RULE

    ?- ai_infer(GM, [q_l-0.5, q_r-0.5], cue_left, Posterior).

Seeing the left cue turns a fifty-fifty belief into certainty. If an
observation is impossible under the current belief, ai_infer fails
honestly instead of inventing a posterior.

EXPECTED FREE ENERGY

For each action, the pack computes G = risk + ambiguity, where risk is
how far the predicted outcomes diverge from the preferred ones, and
ambiguity is how noisy the arrived-at states are. The same quantity
splits another way:

    G = -(pragmatic value + epistemic value)

Pragmatic value is the expected log preference - the pull toward outcomes
the agent wants. Epistemic value is the expected information gain - the
pull toward observations that teach it something. The identity is not a
slogan; the test suite enforces it to six decimal places for every action.

Watch the two drives at work:

    ?- ai_epistemic(GM, [c_l-0.5, c_r-0.5], check, EV).
    EV = 0.6931...           % ln 2 - exactly one bit; the cue is precious

    ?- ai_epistemic(GM, [c_l-1.0], check, EV2).
    EV2 = 0.0                % nothing left to learn - the cue is worthless

    ?- ai_pragmatic(GM, [c_l-1.0], left,  PL).   % PL  = ln 0.85
    ?- ai_pragmatic(GM, [c_l-1.0], right, PR).   % PR  = ln 0.02

When the agent is ignorant, information is valuable. The moment it is
certain, the epistemic pull vanishes and preference takes over. Nobody
programmed an exploration bonus; it is simply what minimizing expected
surprise means.

POLICY SELECTION

    ?- ai_select_policy(GM, [c_l-1.0], 1, 4.0, Policy, P).
    Policy = [left].

Policies (action sequences) are ranked by a softmax over negative expected
free energy, sharpened by a precision Gamma - higher Gamma, more decisive.
ai_act returns the first action of the winner, and ai_step wraps the whole
perceive-then-act cycle: update the belief with the observation, then act
from the updated belief.

    ?- ai_step(GM, [q_l-0.5, q_r-0.5], cue_left, 1, 4.0, Action, Post).
    Action = left.

The agent read the cue, believed it, and went where the reward is. All 23
acceptance tests pass, each numeric expectation hand-computed.
