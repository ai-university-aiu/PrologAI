Chapter 328: Wave 110 - snake_dock (WP-368, Layer 343)

snake_dock solves ARC-AGI-2 task a25697e4. The task gives three
training pairs on 18x25 grids and one test case on a 30x30 grid.
Every scene holds two kinds of objects on a majority-color
background (1 in training, 4 in the test, so every role is read
off each grid rather than memorized). A SNAKE is a single
4-connected piece made of exactly two single-color segments
touching orthogonally. A CONTAINER is one or two nearby wall
components of a single color whose bounding box holds a pocket of
background cells - the SOCKET - open to the outside through a
mouth. Each training grid holds one snake and one container; the
test grid holds two of each at once.

Rule: read the background as the majority color and gather the
per-color 4-connected components; pair two orthogonally touching
components of different colors into a snake and treat every
untouched component as a container wall; cluster same-color walls
within Chebyshev distance two into containers and read each
socket as the background cells inside the cluster's bounding box;
erase every snake; then for each socket in discovery order take
the first snake, segment, and grid isometry - identity, three
rotations, four reflections, in fixed order - whose transformed
segment matches the socket shape exactly and whose partner
segment, carried by the same rigid map, lands entirely on in-grid
background cells without overlapping the socket; fill the socket
and lay the partner segment as the trail through the mouth.

The delicate part is the coloring. Two training grids share the
same snake pixel for pixel and differ only in a mirrored
container, yet the two outputs paint the socket in different
colors. The only property that predicts the paint, across all
five docking events of the task, is the PARITY of the docking
isometry: after a proper rotation each segment keeps its own
color; after a reflection the two segment colors are exchanged -
a snake that must be flipped over to fit shows its underside,
like a physical tile turned face-down. The five events split as
transpose (swap), quarter turn counter-clockwise (keep), pure
translation (keep), quarter turn clockwise (keep), and vertical
flip (swap) - five of five.

Two generalizations matter. A container may be drawn as two
separate wall components with the mouth between them, so
same-color components are clustered by proximity before bounding
boxes are read. And segment size must equal socket size, which
pairs the two snakes to the two containers of the test grid
uniquely before any shape matching runs. The induce clause
dispatches early, directly after pipe_router, requiring equal
input and output dimensions, an identical background, and an
unchanged foreground cell count on the first pair, then verifying
every training pair exactly. The complete rule was written in
plain English covering every grid before any code, all five
docking events were verified cell by cell by hand, a Python
prototype confirmed the algorithm on its first run, and the
integrated rule passed the single-task test and the full
regression on the first compile.

Key predicates (arc_benchmark_2.pl):

  snd_split_snakes_/3  - touching component pairs to snakes
  snd_clusters_/2      - same-color walls to containers
  snd_socket_/4        - background pocket in the cluster box
  snd_erase_snakes_/4  - blank every snake cell
  snd_apply_iso_/3     - one of the eight grid isometries
  snd_norm_/3          - shape normalization to the origin
  snd_match_one_/6     - one docking event in fixed order
  snd_iso_parity_/2    - proper rotation or reflection
  snd_paint_/4         - fill socket and trail
