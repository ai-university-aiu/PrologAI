Chapter 313: Wave 95 - short_circuit (WP-353, Layer 328)

short_circuit solves ARC-AGI-2 task 7b0280bc. The task gives
three training pairs and one test case. The grids are 18x18,
15x15, and 20x20 in training and 30x30 in the test, and the
output always keeps the input size. Every scene draws a wiring
network on a uniform background using exactly three foreground
colors: a terminal color forming exactly two blocks, a marker
color forming small junction blocks, and a body color forming
the wire segments that bend, run diagonally, and branch between
them. In the first training pair the terminals are 1-blocks and
the junctions 2-blocks on 0-wires; in the third pair the roles
flip, with 2-block terminals and 1-block junctions on 6-wires;
in the test the terminals are 3x3 blocks of 0s, the junctions
blocks of 9s, and the wires runs of 1s. The output recolors
exactly one route through the network - the route with the
fewest stops between the two terminals - and leaves everything
else untouched.

Rule: find the fewest-node route between the two terminal
blocks through the graph of same-color components and recolor
its intermediate nodes - wire body cells to 5 and junction
marker cells to 3 - keeping the terminals, the dead-end spurs,
and every longer alternative route unchanged.

Algorithm:

The transform first takes the most common color as the
background with arc2_bg_color_ and ranks the exactly three
foreground colors by ascending cell count with scc_palette_ and
its run-length encoder scc_runs_: the rarest color is the
terminals, the middle one the junction markers, and the most
frequent the wire body. scc_fgcells_ lists every foreground cell
keyed by position, and the Wave 94 helpers sw_parts_ and
sw_flood_ - reused verbatim - group those cells into whole
same-color components under eight-way adjacency. scc_tag_
attaches its color to every component, and the terminal color
must form exactly two components. scc_index_ maps every cell to
its component number, and scc_adj_ builds the adjacency list of
the component graph through scc_neigh_, joining two components
whenever any pair of their cells is eight-adjacent. scc_bfs_
runs a breadth-first search from one terminal component to the
other, expanding whole paths and marking components visited on
enqueue, which yields the route with the fewest nodes. Finally
scc_recolor_ strips the two terminals off the route ends and
paints every cell of every intermediate component with
scc_paint_ and arc2_set_cell_ - 3 when the component carries the
junction marker color, 5 otherwise. The induce clause dispatches
early, directly after seam_weld, requiring equal first-pair
dimensions and exactly four distinct colors in the first input,
then verifying every training pair.

The lesson of the wave is that the network is a genuine graph,
not a pair of disjoint routes. Junction blocks carry three or
more attached segments, dead-end spurs hang directly off the
terminals, and in the test grid one junction block serves both
the shortest route and a long alternative - so no split into
candidate routes can work, and only a breadth-first search over
the component graph picks the right path. Counting nodes is the
right metric: in every pair the recolored route is the unique
fewest-node path between the two terminals, with no cell-count
or Euclidean tie-break needed. A second lesson is that color
roles must be derived per grid: the same color draws the wires
in one pair and the junctions in another, so the rule ranks the
three foreground colors by cell count instead of memorizing
identities.

Key predicates:
  scc_palette_/5       - rank foreground colors by cell count
  scc_runs_/2          - run-length encode a sorted cell list
  scc_fgcells_/3       - list foreground cells keyed by position
  scc_tag_/3           - attach its color to every component
  scc_index_/2         - map every cell to its component number
  scc_adj_/3           - adjacency list of the component graph
  scc_neigh_/4         - components eight-adjacent to one node
  scc_bfs_/5           - fewest-node route between terminals
  scc_recolor_/5       - recolor the intermediate route nodes
  scc_paint_/4         - write one color over listed cells
  arc2_transform(short_circuit, +Grid, -Out)
  arc2_induce_rule(+Pairs, short_circuit)
