Chapter 360: Binding a Layer to Its Stratum - The N6 Layer-to-Stratum Binding

The strict layer rule checks that layers are ordered correctly, not that a layer
matches the thing it represents. The Connectome program builds regions one pack per
Causalontology stratum, and a stratum has an ordinal - a number, read from the
Causalontology structure records, naming how coarse or fine it is. If those packs
are to sit in the right layer order, each pack's layer must track its stratum's
ordinal, and until the N6 binding that alignment was maintained by hand.

You turn the binding on with one extra fact. A stratum-primary pack already declares
its layer with layer(N) in its pack.pl; it now also declares the stratum it
represents, with stratum(Label). The checker bin/check_layer_binding.sh reads each
stratum's ordinal from the authoritative Causalontology records and verifies that
the pack layers are order-preserving with the ordinals: a lower ordinal must not
carry a higher layer, and equal ordinals must share a layer. A pack that declares no
stratum is simply unbound - a gap to fill, never an error - so adoption is
incremental, exactly as with the layer rule.

The point the binding adds over the layer rule alone is that a mis-declared layer can
disguise an upward dependency as a downward one; the binding catches that disguise,
because the coarse stratum was given a lower layer than a finer one, contradicting
the ordinal order the data records. It runs in strict mode (a violation refuses the
load) or reporting mode (a violation is listed without refusing), and is gated in
Continuous Integration (CI). Its honest limit - the ordinal is read from record
artifacts, which could go stale - is filed as Ledger entry N7. The construct is
documented for adopters in docs/layer-binding.md.
