Chapter 298: Wave 80 - ring_dock (WP-338, Layer 313)

ring_dock solves ARC-AGI-2 task b5ca7ac4. The task gives three
training pairs on 22x22 grids and one test case on a 22x25 grid. Each
grid scatters six to eight five-by-five ring objects over a solid
background - every ring is a one-cell uniform border of color 8 or
color 2 around a solid three-by-three uniform interior of some other
color. In the output every 8-bordered ring has slid horizontally into
a parking lane against the left edge and every 2-bordered ring
against the right edge, each ring keeping its rows exactly. Same-side
rings whose rows overlap pack side by side against the ring already
parked nearer the edge, and rings starting on the wrong side of the
grid cross the whole board to reach their lane.

Rule: detect the five-by-five rings, learn which border color goes
left and which goes right, and pack each side's rings against its
edge in edge-nearest order, with collision scoped to same-side rings.

Algorithm:

The background is the most frequent color. Ring detection scans every
feasible top-left corner for a sixteen-cell uniform non-background
border around a nine-cell uniform interior of a different
non-background color, and a total-coverage guard demands that the
grid's non-background cell count equal exactly twenty-five times the
ring count, so the fixed-size detector can never miss content or fire
on a false positive. The rings split by border color into left movers
and right movers - the assignment of the two border colors to the two
directions is the induced rule parameter pair ring_dock(L, R), found
by trying both assignments and keeping the one under which every
training pair transforms correctly. Each side then gets its own fresh
background canvas: left movers sort by ascending column (edge-nearest
first, ties by row) and each in turn slides leftward one column at a
time until the grid edge or an already-parked same-side ring blocks
it, then stamps its five-by-five block; right movers do the mirror
image toward the right edge. Finally the two side canvases merge cell
by cell into the output, preferring non-background cells.

The lesson: collision must be scoped to destination. The first
prototype slid every ring over one shared canvas and failed two of
the three training pairs - a right mover was blocked by a left mover
sitting in its path, even though the training outputs show the two
groups passing straight through each other as they trade sides. One
canvas per side, merged afterward, fixed all three training pairs at
once. A second lesson: sorting each side's movers by distance to
their target edge makes stepwise sliding equivalent to final-position
packing, so the slide loop needs no lookahead and stays glass-box.

Key predicates:
  rd_ring_/3           - detect one five-by-five ring object
  rd_rings_/3          - collect every ring object in the grid
  rd_slide_left_/5     - slide a ring leftward until blocked
  rd_slide_right_/6    - slide a ring rightward until blocked
  rd_place_left_/4     - park all left movers in edge-nearest order
  rd_place_right_/5    - park all right movers in edge-nearest order
  rd_merge_/4          - overlay the two side canvases
  arc2_transform(ring_dock(L, R), +Grid, -Out)
  arc2_induce_rule(+Pairs, ring_dock(L, R))
