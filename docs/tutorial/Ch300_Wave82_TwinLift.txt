Chapter 300: Wave 82 - twin_lift (WP-340, Layer 315)

twin_lift solves ARC-AGI-2 task 62593bfd. The task gives two training
pairs on 28x19 and 30x30 grids and one test case on a 25x25 grid.
Each grid scatters four to six small shapes over a uniform
background, and the background color differs across grids: 0 in the
first training grid, 5 in the second, and 1 in the test grid. In the
output every shape whose cell count matches the cell count of at
least one other shape has floated straight up until flush with the
top edge, and every shape whose cell count is unique among all
shapes has sunk straight down until flush with the bottom edge.
Every shape keeps its columns, its internal form, and its colors.

Rule: count the cells of every 8-connected shape; twins (shapes
sharing a cell count with another shape) float flush to the top
edge, loners (shapes with a unique cell count) sink flush to the
bottom edge, columns unchanged.

Algorithm:

The background is the most frequent color in the grid - counted, not
assumed, because the three grids use three different backgrounds.
Every non-background cell is collected with its coordinates and
color, and the cells are grouped into 8-connected components by
greedy flood fill; connectivity ignores color, so a shape spanning
two colors moves as one unit while two same-colored shapes apart
from each other move independently. The cell count of every
component is recorded in a size census. A component whose size
occurs two or more times in the census shifts up by its topmost row;
a component whose size occurs exactly once shifts down by the grid
height minus one minus its bottommost row; columns never change.
The output grid is rebuilt row by row: cells claimed by a shifted
component take that component's cell colors, and every other cell
takes the background color. The induce clause dispatches early,
directly after wire_relay, requiring preserved dimensions, 8 to 150
foreground cells, at least three components, at least one twin-sized
pair, and at least one unique-sized shape before verifying every
training pair.

The lesson: when a task offers only two training pairs, the
falsifying instance may live in the test input itself. The reference
description of this task speaks of ranking colors by dominance, but
the test grid holds two color-3 shapes that move in opposite
directions - a 12-cell ring that sinks and a 7-cell staircase that
rises - so no per-color rule can be right. Checking every candidate
mechanism against all fifteen shapes visible across the three grids
eliminated positional, symmetry, density, and protrusion stories one
by one and left exactly one survivor: the twin-size census.
