Chapter 302: Wave 84 - motif_shadow (WP-342, Layer 317)

motif_shadow solves ARC-AGI-2 task 64efde09. The task gives two
training pairs on 26x29 and 21x27 grids and one test case on a
29x29 grid, all over background 8. Each input holds two to four
rectangular motifs, each two cells wide and seven or nine cells
long, plus exactly three singleton marker cells sitting on the grid
edges. The motif palettes differ across grids - colors 4, 2, and 3
in the first training input, colors 4, 2, and 1 in the second, and
colors 1, 4, 2, and 3 in the test - but every motif carries a run
of color 2 along exactly one of its long sides.

Rule: each motif's anchor end is the long-axis end farther from its
color-2 run; the three edge markers each align with one motif and
teach an offset-to-color lesson measured from the anchor end, plus
the grid-wide polarity saying whether rays exit on the same cross
side as the 2-run or on the opposite side; every motif then casts
one full ray per lesson, perpendicular to its long axis, from its
exit side out to the grid edge, painting only background cells.

Algorithm:

The background is the most frequent color, and the 8-connected
foreground components split into motif rectangles and singleton
markers; the helpers tw_background_ and tw_components_ from the
twin_lift pack are reused unchanged. A motif rectangle is two cells
wide, at least four cells long, fills its bounding box completely,
and holds color-2 cells on exactly one long side; its anchor end is
the long-axis end farther from that 2-run. A marker on the left or
right grid edge aligns with the vertical motif whose row span holds
the marker row, and a marker on the top or bottom edge aligns with
the horizontal motif whose column span holds the marker column; the
marker teaches the lesson pairing its axis offset from the anchor
end with its color, and votes on the polarity by comparing its side
of the motif with the motif's 2-run side. All votes must agree.
Every motif then casts one ray per deduplicated lesson at the
lesson offset from its own anchor end, from its exit side out to
the grid edge, and the rebuild gives a background cell under a ray
the ray color while every other cell keeps its input value. The
induce clause dispatches early, directly after stripe_cycle,
requiring preserved dimensions, 10 to 200 foreground cells, at
least two parsing motif rectangles, and exactly three singleton
markers before verifying every training pair.

The lesson: the exit direction is a grid parameter, not a motif
parameter. In the first training grid every motif exits on the same
side as its color-2 run, in the second training grid and the test
grid every motif exits on the opposite side, and no structural
feature of the motifs separates the cases - even their palettes
change from grid to grid. The markers decide it: each marker lies
on the ray of its own color, so its side of the aligned motif
reveals the polarity of the whole grid. What looked like an
inconsistent rule became a single parameter read off the input at
answer time, and the rule passed the single-task test and the full
regression on the first compile.
