Chapter 372: What a Mind Expects - Causalontology 4.0.0 and Its Three New Kinds

Causalontology 4.0.0 gives the standard its first records of a MENTAL life, and
PrologAI now declares and passes it: 137 of 137 conformance vectors (V01-V137,
twenty-one schemas), vendored from the causalontology repository at its v4.0.0
tag and run by bin/run_causalontology_conformance.sh. Three new kinds arrive,
and each is a simple idea made first-class.

Plain-language idea. Until now the ontology could record what happens and what
causes what. It could not record what an agent BELIEVES, what an agent EXPECTS,
or how wrong an expectation turned out to be. The three new kinds are exactly
those three records.

A belief attitude (the attitude kind) records what someone's mind contains,
never what is true. It names WHO holds it (a modeled agent inside the ontology,
never a cryptographic signing key - the signer of a statement ABOUT an attitude
is a different thing from its holder), WHICH attitude it is (a closed list:
believes, desires, intends, knows, expects, fears), and WHAT it is about, named
by identity. The content may be flat wrong: a false belief is stored without
raising any conflict, because the mismatch between a mind and the world IS the
interesting fact - that quarantine is what makes a false belief first-class and
shareable. Content may even be another attitude, so "A believes that B believes
X" nests naturally. An attitude carries no strength of its own; how sure we are
OF the attitude is said through the ordinary provenance layer, like any other
claim.

A predicted occurrence (the predicted_occurrence kind) is a forecast. Someone
(the predictor) says an event of some type will happen inside a window,
expressed in exactly ONE notion of time - a calendar (wall-clock) window or an
ordinal tick window, never both and never neither - optionally with a strength
(a confidence for the forecast, identity-bearing when present). A forecast is
deliberately NOT the same record as a report: predicting rain tomorrow and
reporting rain yesterday are different kinds with different identities.

A prediction error (the prediction_error kind) is the grade the world hands
back. It names WHICH prediction is being graded, what was actually OBSERVED
(nothing at all, if the prediction went unfulfilled), and a SIGNED discrepancy -
how far off, and in which direction. An observed event must actually be of the
type the prediction named, or the pairing is flagged. This is the raw material
of learning from surprise: a mind that keeps its forecasts and its errors can
get better.

On the PrologAI side the change is additive and small. The causal_core
vocabulary pack (bumped to 1.1.0) registers the identity-bearing fields of the
three kinds, mints their whole-word identifiers (attitude:, predicted_occurrence:,
prediction_error: - abbreviated schemes are rejected), and enforces the
single-dimension rule on predicted intervals. Nothing about the eighteen earlier
kinds changed, and the frozen identifiers re-pin byte-for-byte, so everything
already stored keeps its identity. Gate: conformance 137/137; pack tests and the
naming gate green; mini regression green: ARC-AGI-1 40/40, ARC-AGI-2 12/12
(10 percent spot-check; full regression deferred).
