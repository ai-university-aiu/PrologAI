Chapter 297: Wave 79 - master_fragment (WP-337, Layer 312)

master_fragment solves ARC-AGI-2 task 269e22fb. The task gives five
training pairs whose inputs range from 8x10 to 16x8 grids (the test
input is 10x10) and whose outputs are all 20x20. Each grid uses
exactly two colors - four pairs use 7 and 8, one pair uses 0 and 3.
Every training output is one shared canonical two-color master
pattern, reproduced up to one of the eight dihedral isometries and
one of the two possible two-color bijections, and every input is a
contiguous rectangular fragment cut directly from its own output. The
task is to recognize where the fragment came from and answer with the
full master pattern rendered in the fragment's orientation and
colors.

Rule: store the first training output as the canonical master; the
answer is the unique candidate among the eight isometries of the
master crossed with the two color bijections that contains the input
fragment as an exact contiguous subgrid.

Algorithm:

Induction stores the first training output verbatim as the canonical
master pattern - the rule's single learned parameter, carried as the
argument of master_fragment(Master). Cheap guards come first: the
output must be at least as tall and as wide as the input and strictly
larger overall, the master must use exactly two colors, and every
training output must share the master dimensions exactly. The
transform then reads the two colors of the fragment and the two
colors of the master and enumerates sixteen full-size candidates: the
eight dihedral isometries of the master - identity, three rotations,
two axis mirrors, and two diagonal mirrors, built from the registry
transforms rotate_90_cw, rotate_180, rotate_90_ccw, reverse_rows,
vertical_flip, and transpose - crossed with the two possible
two-color bijections onto the fragment palette. A candidate survives
only when the fragment appears inside it as an exact contiguous
subgrid, checked by sliding the fragment over every feasible row and
column offset. The surviving candidates are deduplicated with sort/2
and exactly one must remain: that unique candidate is the answer, so
an ambiguous fragment fails rather than guessing. Induction verifies
the transform on every training pair with forall.

The lesson: this is the first wave whose induced rule carries a whole
learned grid as a rule argument, rather than colors or scalar
parameters. The parameterized-rule machinery introduced for
recolor_plus(A, B) extends cleanly to grid-valued parameters: the
early dispatch clause binds the master from the first training output
and the transform treats it as data. A second lesson: the uniqueness
demand doubles as both the correctness proof and the induction
verifier - each of the five training pairs admits exactly one
candidate among sixteen, so the rule is self-checking, and the same
check answered the test case on the first run.

Key predicates:
  mf_colors_/2         - sorted list of the distinct cell colors
  mf_iso_/2            - the eight dihedral isometries of a grid
  mf_recolor_/3        - repaint a grid through a two-color bijection
  mf_subgrid_/2        - fragment appears as a contiguous subgrid
  arc2_transform(master_fragment(Master), +Grid, -Out)
  arc2_induce_rule(+Pairs, master_fragment(Master))
