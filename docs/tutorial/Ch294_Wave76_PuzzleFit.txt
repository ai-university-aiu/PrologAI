Chapter 294: Wave 76 - puzzle_fit (WP-334, Layer 309)

puzzle_fit solves ARC-AGI-2 task 7b3084d4. Each 20x20 input grid
scatters four puzzle pieces over the background - mostly single-color
connected shapes with bumps and notches - and exactly one piece
carries a single anchor stud of color 5 in place of one of its own
cells. The total number of piece cells is always a perfect square:
36, 100, and 16 across the three training grids and 196 in the test
grid. The task is to assemble the pieces into the N-by-N square,
rotating each piece by quarter turns so the pieces interlock with no
gaps and no overlaps, with the color-5 anchor stud landing exactly on
the square's top-left corner.

Rule: the pieces are chiral. Each piece may be turned but never
mirrored, and under that restriction the square has exactly one
assembly.

Algorithm:

The background is the most common cell value. All non-background
cells are grouped into 8-connected components over mixed colors -
8-connected because some piece cells touch only diagonally, and
mixed-color because the anchor stud's color 5 differs from its
piece's body color. Each piece is normalized to its bounding-box
origin, and the summed cell counts must equal N * N, fixing the
output side N. Each piece then generates exactly four variants:
identity and the three quarter-turn rotations, deduplicated by
sorting. Backtracking exact cover fills the board: at each step the
scanline-first empty cell must be covered by the scanline-first cell
of some rotated variant of a still-unplaced piece, fitting inside the
board and overlapping only empty cells. The accepted solution is the
tiling whose top-left corner carries the color-5 anchor stud, and the
placed cells are painted row by row into the output square, every
cell keeping its input color. The induction pre-filter requires the
first output to be strictly smaller than the first input in both
dimensions, exactly square, with color 5 in its top-left cell and not
in its bottom-left cell, then verifies all pairs with forall.

The lesson: reflections do not belong to this puzzle. Under the full
dihedral group of eight variants per piece the grids admit two or
three perfect tilings each and the first-found tiling fails the
hidden test; under rotations only, every grid - training and test
alike - admits exactly one perfect tiling, confirmed by exhaustive
enumeration. A second lesson came from the prototype: the
counterclockwise rotation map reused the input column variable as the
output column, and the resulting zero-solution search on a grid that
had been solved by hand exposed the bug - which the singleton-variable
warning had flagged all along.

  pf_variants_/2       - the four quarter-turn rotations, deduped
