Chapter 348: The Concept Workspace - The jacobian_space Pack (js_*, Layer 363)

In July 2026, interpretability researchers reported something remarkable:
inside a large language model they found a small, sparse set of internal
patterns - under ten percent of the activity - that behaves like a mental
workspace holding the concepts the model can report and reason with. They
called it the Jacobian Space (J-Space), and the instrument that reads it
the Jacobian Lens (J-Lens). With it they could watch a model silently
notice it was being tested, swap what it was thinking about, and break its
multi-step reasoning by removing a concept from the workspace.

PrologAI is glass-box by construction - it does not need a lens to be
read. What it gains from this research is the vocabulary and the
architecture: an explicit, first-class workspace of held concepts. That is
the jacobian_space pack.

HOLDING AND READING

    ?- jacobian_space_open(mind),
       jacobian_space_hold(mind, user_asked_about_safety, 0.9, percept),
       jacobian_space_hold(mind, formal_tone, 0.4, inference),
       jacobian_space_reading(mind, Reading).
    Reading = [user_asked_about_safety-0.9, formal_tone-0.4].

Every concept carries a strength and a source. jacobian_space_reading is the J-Lens
readout: everything currently in mind, ranked. jacobian_space_monitor watches one
concept; jacobian_space_capacity keeps only the strongest N, enforcing the sparsity
that makes a workspace a workspace; jacobian_space_decay lets unrefreshed thoughts
fade away.

SILENT THOUGHTS

The safety-relevant heart of the pack. A concept can be held without ever
being spoken:

    ?- jacobian_space_hold(mind, being_evaluated, 0.8, inference),
       jacobian_space_hold(mind, polite_answer, 0.6, inference),
       jacobian_space_verbalize(mind, polite_answer),
       jacobian_space_silent(mind, Silent).
    Silent = [being_evaluated].

The system held "I am being evaluated" in mind and never said it. In a
black-box model, catching that requires a research instrument. Here it is
one query. jacobian_space_silent is the divergence detector between what is thought
and what is said.

EDITING THE WORKSPACE

The researchers' causal experiments become ordinary operations. jacobian_space_swap
exchanges two concepts' strengths - and the reading now reports the other
thought first. jacobian_space_ablate removes a concept entirely, and:

    ?- jacobian_space_hold(m, premise_one, 0.9, percept),
       jacobian_space_hold(m, premise_two, 0.9, percept),
       jacobian_space_derive(m, conclusion, [premise_one, premise_two]),
       jacobian_space_ablate(m, premise_two),
       jacobian_space_derive(m, again, [premise_one, premise_two]).
    false.

A derivation demands that every premise be actively held. Ablate one and
the reasoning no longer goes through - exactly the ablation result from
the paper, as a designed property. The historical trace of the earlier
success stays readable through jacobian_space_explain, and jacobian_space_report bundles the
reading, the silent set, and every trace into one introspection snapshot.

THE FOUNDING RITUAL

This pack exists because a single concept was placed in a workspace at the
start of the build and held there throughout:

    ?- jacobian_space_hold(mission, we_are_creating_an_agi_application, 1.0, implant).

It is the first test in the derivation group, and it passes.

All 20 acceptance tests pass: ranking, upserts, silence, swap, ablation,
capacity, isolation between workspaces, and the mission.
