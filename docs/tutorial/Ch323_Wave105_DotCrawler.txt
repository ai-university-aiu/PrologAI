Chapter 323: Wave 105 - dot_crawler (WP-363, Layer 338)

dot_crawler solves ARC-AGI-2 task 5545f144. The task gives three
training pairs and one test case. The training inputs are 10x26,
8x27, and 12x25 grids split into two, four, and two equal panels
by full-height columns of a single non-background color; the
test input is a single 15x15 panel with no separators at all,
and the output always keeps one panel's size. Each panel shows
cells of one foreground color: a multi-cell shape - the
crawler - plus a sprinkling of single cells - the dots. The
panels are not layers to be combined. They are TIME: successive
snapshots of one crawl. The dots are a route whose consecutive
stops always share a row or a column, the crawler's protruding
nose cell rides exactly on top of each visited dot, and visited
dots vanish from later panels. The output shows only the final
frame: a clean background panel with the crawler docked
nose-first on the last dot of the route, its body rotated by
the quarter turn that points the nose along the final leg.

Rule: read the background as the most frequent color; split the
grid at full-height uniform non-background columns and keep the
leftmost panel; group the panel's foreground into 8-connected
components - the largest is the crawler, the single cells are
the dots; find the nose as the shape cell sitting alone on one
edge of the shape's bounding box whose outward ray hits a dot
and whose greedy nearest-aligned-dot chain consumes every dot;
dock the crawler on the final chain dot, nose exactly on the
dot, body offsets rotated from the nose heading onto the
direction of the last leg.

Algorithm:

The transform first calls td_background_, which flattens the
grid, counts the palette with clumped, and takes the most
frequent color as the background. dc_panels_ finds every
separator via dc_sep_col_ - a column whose cells all carry one
uniform non-background color - and cuts the columns into ranges
with dc_ranges_ and dc_slice_, keeping the leftmost panel as
the scene and its dimensions as the output size. bt_fg_cells_
collects the panel's foreground cells, which must all share one
color, and dc_comps8_ with dc_grow8_ groups their positions
into 8-connected components by flood fill over king-move
neighbors; dc_by_size_ ranks them so the largest component is
the crawler and every single-cell component is a dot.
dc_nose_cand_ enumerates the nose candidates: for each of up,
down, left, and right, dc_extreme_cell_ takes the shape cells
on that edge of the bounding box computed by bt_bbox_ and
admits the cell only when it is alone there. dc_first_dot_
fires the outward ray and keys the in-line dots by distance,
dc_nearest_ picks the closest, and dc_chain_ then greedily hops
to the nearest remaining dot sharing the current row or column
until the pool is empty - a candidate is valid only when the
chain consumes every dot. When two candidates survive (the
chain read forward and backward), dc_pick_nose_ consults the
second panel through dc_panel_dots_: the true first stop is
already consumed or covered by the shape there, while the false
one still sits free as a dot. Docking is pure geometry:
dc_arrival_ and dc_dir_ read the direction of the last chain
leg, dc_turns_ counts the clockwise quarter turns from the nose
heading onto it via dc_cw_, dc_rot_offs_ rotates the shape's
nose-relative offsets by that many turns, and the rotated cells
are painted around the final dot on a bt_blank_ background
canvas with arc2_set_cell_. The induce clause dispatches early,
directly after box_tally, requiring equal first-pair heights,
an output width equal to the leftmost panel width, a single
foreground color, a crawler of at least three cells, and only
single-cell siblings, then verifying every training pair.

The lesson of the wave is to account for every dot before
theorizing. The task had been shelved since Wave 90 under a
wrong panel-overlay reading; the key that opened it was noticing
that the dots form an orthogonal chain from the shape's tip and
that the panels are successive snapshots of the walk, so the
test grid - a single frame with no separators - is solved by
exactly the same reading. The discriminating observation is the
full-consumption nose test: the second training grid presents
two lone edge cells whose chains are exact reverses of each
other, and only the second-panel snapshot separates the route's
true start from its end. The rule passed the single-task test
and the full regression on the first compile.
