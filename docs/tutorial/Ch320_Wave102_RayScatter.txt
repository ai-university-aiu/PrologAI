Chapter 320: Wave 102 - ray_scatter (WP-360, Layer 335)

ray_scatter solves ARC-AGI-2 task 4a21e3da. The task gives
three training pairs and one test case. The training grids are
18x18 and the test grid is 20x20, and the output always keeps
the input size. Every input shows exactly three colors: a
uniform background, one connected blob of a single color
sitting near the middle of the grid, and one or two lone marker
cells resting on the grid border - on the top and right edges
in the first pair, the top edge in the second, and the bottom
edge in the third and in the test. In the output the blob has
been blasted apart. Each marker has fired a ray into the grid
along its own row or column, reaching exactly as far as the
farthest blob cell on that line; the background cells along the
ray are painted the marker color, while the blob cells lying on
the line keep their color and stay welded in place. Every other
blob cell has flown away: the blob's side pieces have
translated rigidly into the corners of the grid, each landing
flush against two walls with its shape preserved cell for cell
- and in the two-ray pair, the bottom-left piece has vanished
entirely.

Rule: rank the three colors by frequency into background, blob,
and marker; fire one ray per border marker to the farthest blob
cell on its line, painting background positions marker-colored;
anchor the on-line blob cells; split the rest into quadrant or
side pieces; translate each piece flush into its corner - away
from each ray across the line, toward the marker's edge along a
single ray - and delete any piece whose corner touches no
marker edge.

Algorithm:

The transform first calls rs_scene_, which flattens the grid,
counts the palette with clumped, and requires exactly three
colors ranked by frequency - the background, the blob, and a
marker color of at most two cells - collecting the marker and
blob cell positions. rs_rays_ and rs_ray_ turn each border
marker into a directed ray term: vertical firing downward from
the top row or upward from the bottom row, horizontal firing
rightward from the left column or leftward from the right
column. rs_ray_far_ collects the blob cells on each ray's line
and takes the farthest one in the travel direction as the
stopping point, and rs_draw_ray_ with rs_ray_span_ and
rs_ray_cell_ paints every line position between the marker and
the stop - blob positions keep the blob color, background
positions take the marker color. rs_on_ray_ then anchors every
blob cell whose column matches a vertical ray or whose row
matches a horizontal ray; these cells never move. The remaining
movable cells are grouped by rs_groups_ with rs_key_: against a
horizontal ray the row side picks the vertical half, otherwise
the vertical marker's own edge does, and against a vertical ray
the column side picks the horizontal half, otherwise the
horizontal marker's edge does - giving the four quadrants
around a two-ray crossing or the two sides of a single line.
Finally rs_place_ lands each group: rs_corner_edges_ names the
two walls of the destination corner, and the group survives
only if one of them holds a marker (rs_marker_edges_);
rs_offsets_ computes the rigid shift that puts the group's
bounding box flush against both walls, and rs_shift_put_ paints
the translated cells in the blob color. The induce clause
dispatches early, directly after legend_snake, requiring equal
first-pair dimensions, exactly three colors with the rarest on
the border, and every ray line crossing the blob, then
verifying every training pair.

The lesson of the wave is to re-read the data before
theorizing. A first reading of the second training pair
invented a phantom missing cell, and three cell-deletion
hypotheses died against clean counterexamples before a careful
re-print of the expected output showed the piece intact all
along. The real anomaly was one whole missing quadrant,
explained by one clean test: the destination corner must touch
an edge that holds a marker. The rest of the task is plain
bookkeeping - anchored line cells never move, and every other
cell rides its piece's rigid translation into a corner.

Key predicates:
  rs_scene_/6          - rank colors, collect markers and blob
  rs_rays_/4           - one directed ray term per marker
  rs_ray_far_/3        - farthest blob cell on a ray line
  rs_draw_ray_/6       - paint one ray marker-colored over bg
  rs_on_ray_/2         - anchor blob cells on a ray line
  rs_groups_/3         - quadrant or side pieces of the blob
  rs_marker_edges_/4   - border edges holding markers
  rs_place_/7          - corner survival test and placement
  rs_offsets_/7        - corner-flush rigid translation
  rs_shift_put_/6      - paint one translated piece cell
  arc2_transform(ray_scatter, +Grid, -Out)
  arc2_induce_rule(+Pairs, ray_scatter)
