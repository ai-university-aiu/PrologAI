================================================================================
Chapter 357: The Fullest Set - Imagination, Priming, Regulation, and Lore
(co_*, Layers 396 through 399)
================================================================================

By the end of Chapter 356 the Causalontology family already spanned every major
faculty a body of work on building minds (referred to internally as
THE_BUILDING_FILES) describes. This chapter adds the four packs that fill the
last partial gaps - the ones that were only touched, not fully covered - so the
family reaches genuine faculty-completeness. As always each pack is
self-contained, glass-box, commented line by line, tested, and given a new name
rather than borrowing the source's terms. Every co_ pack lives in the PrologAI
repository at github.com/ai-university-aiu/PrologAI.

This chapter also records a piece of housekeeping done in the same wave: the six
packs that still used a hand-rolled test printer were converted to the standard
PLUnit test framework, and seven packs were tightened so that every line of code
carries an English comment. That change touched only comments and test scaffolding
- no behaviour changed - and it was checked mechanically to be sure.


co_muse - Imagination (WP-421, Layer 396)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A mind should be able to think before it acts: to try an action in its head,
watch what would happen, and keep the useful discoveries - all without touching
the real world. The danger is confusing the imagined with the real, so co_muse
keeps several parallel "realities": what is observed (actual fact), what is
desired (goals), what is expected (predictions), what is imagined (scenarios
being tried), and what is recalled (from memory). To imagine, it rolls a starting
state forward through a set of known transitions, recording each state it visits
in the imagined reality only. A "what if" is therefore explored fully yet stays
sealed off from fact. co_muse can score whether an imagined path reaches a goal
and pick the candidate plan that reaches it in the fewest steps. Crucially, a
finding crosses from imagined into observed reality only by a deliberate
promotion - never by the act of imagining - so a hypothetical can never quietly
become a belief. This is the part world_model's in-model rollout did not provide: the
quarantine and the discipline.


priming - Priming (WP-422, Layer 397)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Think of how the word "salt" brings "pepper" to mind. That is priming: when
something becomes active, the things associated with it become relevant too, more
weakly the further away they are. priming holds a graph of weighted
associations, and from a set of active starting nodes it lets activation flow
outward along the links, fading by a decay at each hop. A node's activation is
the strongest path that reaches it. The calculation is a plain, terminating
relaxation - never a trained weight - so you can always read why a node is
primed. priming is the natural partner of co_salience from Chapter 355:
salience picks the single most important item from the things offered to the
mind, while priming decides what becomes relevant, and available to be offered,
in the first place.


regulation - Regulation (WP-423, Layer 398)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
When a behaviour gets feedback, the feedback comes in four flavours, set by
whether the result was expected and whether it was good: a success you predicted
is confirming, a failure you predicted is disappointing, a success you did not
predict is serendipitous, and a failure you did not predict is shocking. regulation
tracks a behaviour's reliability and names each new result this way. Its deeper
trick is discrimination learning. When one behaviour both succeeds and fails, the
right fix is not to water it down but to split it: find the feature of the
situation that is present in every win and no loss (or every loss and no win) and
refine the rule to require that feature, or to shun it. co_learn from the early
chapters induces causal relations and co_gauge from Chapter 355 watches whole
strategies; regulation works at the level of a single behaviour, sharpening it.


lore - Lore (WP-424, Layer 399)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A mind should not only remember what happened but learn the lesson of it.
lore records experiences as a situation, the response taken, and how it turned
out. From a pile of these it notices a theme - a situation-pattern that keeps
recurring - recalls the lessons of past situations whose pattern is present now,
ranks the responses by how often they turned out good, and distils a maxim: do
the response that usually works, or, if none does, avoid the one most tied to a
bad result. Where episodic_memory stores individual experience-cases and concept_formation groups
objects into concepts, lore sits above experience and turns repetition into
standing advice.


Where the family stands now
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
With imagination, priming, regulation, and lore in place, the Causalontology
family is faculty-complete against THE_BUILDING_FILES: it perceives, remembers
and forgets, attends and is primed, is motivated and feels, reasons causally,
hypothesises and verifies, models and imagines the world, plans and repairs,
watches itself, keeps itself safe, understands other minds, grounds language,
draws analogies, forms concepts, regulates its behaviours, and learns the lessons
of its own history. What began as a process-first causal ontology that could play
an interactive game is now a broad, honest, glass-box cognitive architecture - a
new synthetic-mind substrate, built in PrologAI.
