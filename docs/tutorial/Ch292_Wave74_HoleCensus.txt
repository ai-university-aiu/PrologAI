Chapter 292: Wave 74 - hole_census (WP-332, Layer 307)

hole_census solves ARC-AGI-2 task 8698868d. Each input grid (20x15 to
30x30) holds two families of rectangular objects over a background.
The first family is a set of equally sized large tiles of distinct
colors packed side by side into a row-and-column layout. The second
family is an equal number of smaller rectangular shapes of their own
colors scattered elsewhere over the background. Both families are
pierced by background-colored holes: the tiles carry a few scattered
single-cell holes, while the shapes carry hole patterns that clump
into blocks, bars, and diagonals. The task is to redraw the packed
tile layout alone, each tile solid in its own color with one small
shape stamped dead center inside it, the shape's holes filled by the
tile color.

Rule: the holes are a census. A tile with N scattered holes belongs
to the shape whose background-hole region forms exactly N
4-connected components - the hole count is the invariant carried
from shape interior to tile face.

Algorithm:
(1) Background and components: the background is the most common cell
    value (arc2_bg_color_/2); every non-background cell is collected
    and partitioned into same-color 4-connected components
    (bst_components_/2, reused from band_stamp).
(2) Census annotation: each component is annotated with its bounding
    box (hc_bbox4_/5, reused from hole_color) and its hole census -
    the number of 4-connected components formed by the background
    cells inside its bounding box (hcs_items_/4, hcs_census_/7).
(3) Tile and shape split: the components whose bounding-box area
    equals the maximum are the tiles; all remaining components are
    the shapes; tile and shape counts must be equal, every tile must
    share one bounding-box size, and every shape must share one
    strictly smaller size in both dimensions.
(4) Census pairing: each tile is paired with the unique shape whose
    hole census equals the tile's own hole census (hcs_pairing_/3,
    hcs_match_/3); exactly one shape may match each tile, and the
    pairing must use every shape exactly once - a verified bijection.
(5) Render: the output spans exactly the packed tile layout, anchored
    at the smallest tile top row and left column; each tile is redrawn
    solid in its own color, its paired shape is stamped centered
    inside it, shape cells keep the shape color, and shape holes take
    the tile color (hcs_render_/14).
(6) Induction pre-filter: the first training pair's output must have
    strictly fewer rows and strictly fewer columns than its input;
    then all training pairs are verified with forall.

The decisive observation is that two simpler readings both fail.
Counting raw hole cells fails because a shape's holes clump: a 3x3
block of nine hole cells is one component, and its partner tile
carries one hole, not nine. Matching hole positions fails because the
tile holes sit at arbitrary spots that do not align with the shape's
hole pattern when the shape is centered. Only the component count of
the holes survives. The bijection check turns any ambiguous grid into
a clean rule failure instead of a wrong stamp. Both training pairs
and the test case pass on the first run.

Key predicates:
  hcs_items_/4         - annotate components with bounding box and census
  hcs_census_/7        - count hole components inside a bounding box
  hcs_pairing_/3       - bijective tile-shape pairing by equal census
  hcs_match_/3         - the unique shape carrying a given census
  hcs_render_/14       - paint the packed layout with centered shapes
  bst_components_/2    - same-color 4-connected components (reused)
  hc_bbox4_/5          - component bounding box (reused)
  arc2_bg_color_/2     - most common cell value as background (reused)
  arc2_transform(hole_census, +Grid, -Out)
  arc2_induce_rule(+Pairs, hole_census)

Task solved: 8698868d (2 training pairs + 1 test case).
Score: 70/120 = 58.33% to 71/120 = 59.17%.
