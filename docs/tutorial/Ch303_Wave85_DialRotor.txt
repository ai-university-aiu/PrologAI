Chapter 303: Wave 85 - dial_rotor (WP-343, Layer 318)

dial_rotor solves ARC-AGI-2 task 6ffbe589. The task gives three
training pairs and one test case, all on 20x20 inputs over
background 0, with square outputs of 13x13, 10x10, 10x10, and
13x13. Each input holds one main figure - a framed square wrapped
in a dotted ring - plus two or three small straight pieces lying
far away from the figure. The palettes vary per grid: ring 3 with
core colors 8 and 6 in the first training input, ring 5 with core
colors 3 and 4 in the second, ring 4 with core colors 1 and 2 in
the third, and ring 5 with core colors 3 and 4 again in the test.

Rule: the main figure is cropped together with every component
within Chebyshev distance 3 of its bounding box; each far-away
piece is a dial - a straight single-color run of color C with
length L commanding "rotate color C's layer by L quarter turns
clockwise" (L mod 4); the ring color is the unique color on the
crop's outermost layer and the ring thickness is the minimum inset
of any non-ring-colored cell; the ring zone spins by the ring
color's dial (staying put without one) and the core block spins by
the largest dial among its own colors.

Algorithm:

The non-zero cells are grouped into 8-connected components with the
reused helper tw_components_ from the twin_lift pack, and the
component with the largest bounding box is the main figure. Every
component with a cell within Chebyshev distance 3 of that bounding
box merges into the crop box - this pulls in the detached dotted-
ring pieces of the test grid - and every farther component is a
dial piece parsed by dr_run_ as a straight single-color run. The
crop must be square. The ring color is read from the crop's
outermost layer, the thickness K is the minimum inset of any
non-ring-colored cell, and the core block is the central region
inset by K. The ring zone rotates by the ring color's dial and the
core block by the largest dial among the core's colors; dr_rotn_
delegates the quarter turns to the existing rotate_90_cw,
rotate_180, and rotate_90_ccw named rules, and dr_row_ pastes the
rotated core back into the center of the spun crop. The induce
clause dispatches early, directly after motif_shadow, requiring a
strictly smaller square output, 10 to 300 foreground cells, and at
least two components before verifying every training pair.

The lesson: the palette does not pick the rotation. The second
training grid and the test grid share the exact same colors - ring
5 with core 3 and 4 - yet one needs a core quarter turn and the
other a core half turn, so any variant-by-palette reading is a red
herring. The debris pieces that look like noise are the actual
instruction stream: their lengths are dials, and the test's
five-cell ring dial proves the modulo-4 reading, one full
revolution plus a quarter. The decisive method was to brute-force
the ring and core transform pairs per training pair first and hunt
for the selecting input feature second: length-of-piece matched all
four grids with zero contradictions, and the rule passed the
single-task test and the full regression on the first compile.
