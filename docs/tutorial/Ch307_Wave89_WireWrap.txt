Chapter 307: Wave 89 - wire_wrap (WP-347, Layer 322)

wire_wrap solves ARC-AGI-2 task 88bcf3b4. The task gives five
training pairs and one test case, all with same-size inputs and
outputs, on grids from 7x7 up to 22x22. Each grid holds one or more
three-color systems on a uniform background: a straight orthogonal
line segment, a crumpled meandering wire attached orthogonally to
one endpoint of the line, and a separate marker shape - a single
cell, a short bar, a zigzag, or a staircase blob. The output keeps
the line and the marker exactly where they are, erases the crumpled
wire, and re-lays it as a clean path that leaves the line end, runs
diagonally to the marker, bends snugly around the marker's near
face, and departs on the mirrored diagonal.

Rule: the re-laid wire is pure geometry - a diagonal approach from
the attachment cell toward the marker, clamped to the marker's near
lane; a hug laying one cell beyond each marker row's or column's
near edge from the contact side to the far side; and a mirrored
45-degree departure ray - truncated at the input wire's exact cell
count or the grid edge, whichever comes first.

Algorithm:

The majority color is the background with the reused helper
tw_background_, and ww_groups_ collects one component per remaining
color, each verified as a single 8-connected set by ww_connected_
over the reused tw_components_. ww_straight_ recognizes lines as
straight orthogonal segments of at least three cells, ww_ends_
yields their endpoints, and ww_orth_ pairs each line with the
crumpled wire whose cell touches an endpoint orthogonally; the
leftover components are markers, and ww_nearest_ gives each system
the marker nearest to its attachment endpoint by Chebyshev
distance. ww_sign_ takes, per axis, the sign of the marker centroid
minus the line endpoint - a zero component is tie-broken by the
side where the input wire's mass lies - and ww_approach_ walks that
diagonal from the attachment cell, each axis clamped to the lane
one cell outside the marker's bounding box, until the path is
8-adjacent to the marker. ww_ctype_ classifies the contact as a row
contact (marker cell ahead along the row axis, directly or across
the diagonal) or a column contact (marker cell aside along the
column axis), and ww_face_ lays the hug cells one column beyond
each marker row's near edge or one row beyond each marker column's
near edge, ordered from the contact side to the far side. ww_ray_
casts the departure ray with the lateral component of the approach
diagonal reversed, ww_ingrid_prefix_ cuts the chained path at the
first off-grid cell, the path is truncated to the input wire's cell
count, and the reused tt_render_ paints lines, markers, and re-laid
wires onto a fresh background canvas. The induce clause dispatches
early, directly after band_jigsaw, requiring preserved dimensions,
6 to 250 foreground cells, and a distinct foreground color count
that is a positive multiple of three of at most nine before
verifying every training pair.

The lesson: the wire's length is not always conserved - two of the
five training pairs lose wire cells because the re-laid path hits
the grid edge before the length is spent, which falsifies every
rigid-transform, rotation, and length-preserving reading of the
task. The wire is not moved; it is recomputed, and the input wire
contributes only its color, its cell count, its attachment cell,
and a tie-break side. Hand-tracing all seven line-wire-marker
systems (five training, two in the test) before coding exposed the
same four phases every time, and the face-based hug follows zigzag
and staircase contours with no special cases where contour-walk and
taut-string formulations kept failing. The rule passed all five
training pairs and the test case on the first prototype run and the
full regression on the first compile.
