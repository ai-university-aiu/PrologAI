================================================================================
Chapter 356: The Next Wave - Grounding, Other Minds, Analogy, and Concepts
(co_*, Layers 392 through 395)
================================================================================

The Cognitive Completion suite of Chapter 355 named two faculties it left for a
later wave - a Causalontology-native way to understand everyday clues, and a
Causalontology-native way to model other minds - and it did so honestly rather
than pretending the family was finished. This chapter closes those two gaps and
adds two more faculties that a body of work on building minds (referred to
internally as THE_BUILDING_FILES) treats as core modes of thought: analogy and
concept formation. As always, every pack is self-contained, glass-box, commented
line by line, tested, and given a new name of its own rather than borrowing the
source's terms. Every co_ pack lives in the PrologAI repository at
github.com/ai-university-aiu/PrologAI.


grounding - Clue Grounding (WP-417, Layer 392)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Causalontology_v5 describes an application where a person watches a game and
gives Mentova clues - "that looks like a key," "pick that up," "don't touch
that" - and each clue teaches the system something. The document named the seam
that turns a clue into the system's own terms grounding, but the pack had not yet
been built. This is it. A clue is a phrase or a list of words; a referent is
whatever "that" or "there" points at - a salient object, or a cell the person
clicked. grounding maps the clue to a small, fixed, inspectable list of
assertions: a key becomes a key-like thing that can be picked up and opens locks;
a lock becomes a thing openable by a key and a goal to open it; a door becomes a
goal to walk through; a warning marks an action as one to avoid; praise becomes
positive reinforcement. Each grounded hint is marked high-confidence but
defeasible, so if acting later contradicts the clue, the system can revise it -
an honest mistake in a clue is corrected by evidence rather than frozen in.


theory_of_mind - Theory of Mind (WP-418, Layer 393)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A complete mind must be able to think about other minds. theory_of_mind does two things.
First, it works out what another agent wants by watching it move: you tell the
pack which places the agent might be heading to and report its steps, and it
picks the target the agent's moves most consistently get closer to, then predicts
the agent's next step toward that goal. Second, it keeps what an agent believes
separate from what is actually true, so it can point to a false belief - a thing
the agent believes that the real state contradicts. This is the classic
Sally-Anne test of theory of mind: Sally believes the ball is where she left it,
though it has quietly been moved, and a mind with theory of mind can represent
that Sally is mistaken. theory_of_mind is the Causalontology-native version of that
ability; the wider system already had a separate, non-Causalontology one.


analogy - Analogy (WP-419, Layer 394)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Analogy is seeing that two different situations share the same shape, and
carrying a lesson from one to the other. analogy takes two situations, each
written as a set of relations between objects (the same shape the object_relations pack
produces), and finds the way of matching up their objects that makes the most
relations line up. It scores how good the match is, and once it has the match it
can transfer a fact - a rule, a goal, a disposition - from the first situation to
the second. The textbook example is the solar system and the atom: analogy maps
the sun to the nucleus and the planets to the electrons, and carries "the sun
pulls the planet" across to "the nucleus pulls the electron." That is analogy
made into plain, inspectable rules.


concept_formation - Concept Formation (WP-420, Layer 395)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A mind should grow its own vocabulary of concepts rather than being handed all of
them. concept_formation watches a stream of things, each described by a bundle of
features, and notices which of them share enough features to deserve a common
name. It clusters them in a simple, transparent way - each ungrouped thing starts
a group and pulls in every later thing that keeps the group's shared features
above a threshold - and coins a concept from the shared core: the core is the
concept's definition and the members are its examples. A new thing can then be
classified into a concept when it has all of the concept's defining features. Show
it several fruits and it coins "sweet fruit"; show it a brick alongside them and
the brick, sharing too little, is left out. That is category learning as
glass-box clustering.


Where the family stands now
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
With these four packs the Causalontology family spans every major faculty in
THE_BUILDING_FILES, from perception through memory, attention, motivation,
feeling, reasoning, prediction, planning, learning, metacognition, compensation,
safety, and forgetting, to social cognition, language grounding, analogy, and
concept formation. What began as a process-first causal ontology that could play
an interactive game has become a broad, honest, glass-box cognitive architecture.
