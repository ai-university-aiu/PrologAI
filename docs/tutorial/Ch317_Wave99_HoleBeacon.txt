Chapter 317: Wave 99 - hole_beacon (WP-357, Layer 332)

hole_beacon solves ARC-AGI-2 task de809cff. The task gives two
training pairs and one test case. The grids are 20x20 in
training and 30x30 in the test, and the output always keeps the
input size. Every input holds two large rectilinear regions of
two distinct colors on a zero background - 3 and 4 in the first
pair, 1 and 2 in the second, 1 and 6 in the test - disturbed by
three kinds of single-cell noise: background holes punched
inside the regions, wrong-colored cells embedded inside the
regions, and colored strays scattered over the background. The
output cleans all three kinds of noise and marks every hole
with a 3x3 beacon whose ring takes the RIVAL region's color and
whose center is painted 8; the test grid carries twenty-four
beacons, some overlapping each other's rings.

Rule: label every cell region A, region B, or background by a
majority vote over its orthogonal cross neighborhood at
distances one and two, ties broken by the cell's own color;
run the identical vote a second time over the first pass's
labels; erase strays, repair wrong colors, and stamp every
background hole inside a region as a beacon - a rival-colored
3x3 ring around an 8 center, centers painted after all rings.

Algorithm:

The transform first reads the palette: hb_colors_ flattens the
grid and sorts it, requiring exactly [0, A, B] and binding the
two region colors. hb_label_pass_ then labels every cell:
hb_vote_ collects the values of the eight in-bounds cross
probes enumerated by hb_probe_ - one and two steps up, down,
left, and right - and tallies the votes for A, for B, and for
the background. A unique maximum labels the cell directly; on a
tie the cell keeps its own input color when that color is among
the tied leaders and falls to background otherwise, which is
what lets a region cell at a convex corner survive with three
same-color neighbors while a colored bump on a straight edge is
erased with the same three. The same pass then runs a second
time, voting over the first pass's labels instead of the raw
colors - strays that the first pass silenced to background now
stay silent in every neighbor's vote. The final label grid is
itself the base output: background labels erase strays, region
labels repair wrong-colored cells and keep clean cells.
hb_centers_ then collects every cell whose input color is zero
yet whose label names a region - the holes - and hb_ring_ folds
over them, painting the rival region's color over every
in-bounds cell of each 3x3 stamp area through hb_paint_, which
clips at the grid edges. hb_center_ folds over the centers last
and paints each one 8, so adjacent beacons whose rings overlap
each other's centers keep every center visible. The induce
clause dispatches early, directly after twin_beam, requiring
equal first-pair dimensions, a first-input palette of exactly
zero plus two colors, and an 8 in the first output, then
verifying every training pair.

The lesson of the wave is that denoising by local majority is
fragile exactly where the noise clusters. On the test grid two
stray cells sit two steps from a background cell near the
region border, and the raw-color vote tips four to three toward
the region - stamping a beacon where none belongs. A
distance-weighted vote repairs the test but breaks both
training pairs at region corners. The cure is the second vote
pass: after pass one the strays already carry background
labels, so the revote falls to background and the spurious
beacon never appears. This is the Wave 98 non-local-state
lesson in reverse - here the transform's own intermediate
labels are the state that separates two locally identical
votes.

Key predicates:
  hb_colors_/3         - palette read: exactly [0, A, B]
  hb_label_pass_/5     - one full cross-vote labeling pass
  hb_probe_/2          - the eight cross offsets at 1 and 2
  hb_vote_/7           - majority label with own-color tie-break
  hb_centers_/3        - input-zero cells labeled as a region
  hb_ring_/5           - stamp one rival-colored 3x3 ring
  hb_paint_/4          - paint one in-bounds cell, clip outside
  hb_center_/4         - paint one beacon center 8
  arc2_transform(hole_beacon, +Grid, -Out)
  arc2_induce_rule(+Pairs, hole_beacon)
