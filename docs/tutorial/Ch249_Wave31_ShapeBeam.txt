Chapter 249: Wave 31 - shape_beam (WP-289, Layer 264)

WHAT IT DOES

shape_beam solves ARC-AGI-2 task 5961cc34. A rod (a single value-4 cell
adjacent to a trail of value-2 cells) fires a beam in the direction opposite
its trail. The beam travels through connected shapes made of value-1 body
cells and value-3 exit-marker cells. Each shape the beam enters is converted
entirely to value 2, and the beam is redirected toward the open (background)
side of the exit markers. This relay continues until the beam exits the grid.
Shapes not reached by any beam are erased; the output contains only value 2
on a background.

PREDICATES

  arc2_transform(shape_beam, +Grid, -Out)
      Entry point. Guards: exactly one value-4 cell; at least one value-1
      cell. Identifies background BG, rod cell (RR, RC) and beam direction
      (BeamDir), collects shape components, classifies each shape, and runs
      the beam relay before painting the result.

  arc2_sb_bg_(+Grid, -BG)
      Identifies the background as the most-frequent cell value.

  arc2_sb_rod_(+Grid, -RR, -RC, -BeamDir)
      Finds the value-4 cell and its adjacent value-2 trail. BeamDir is
      opposite the trail direction.

  arc2_sb_rod_cells_(+Grid, +RR, +RC, +BeamDir, -RodCells)
      Collects the value-4 cell and all value-2 trail cells as R-C pairs.

  arc2_sb_comps_(+Positions, -Components)
      Partitions a list of R-C positions into 4-connected components using
      BFS seed expansion.

  arc2_sb_classify_(+Grid, +BG, +Comp, -shape(AllCells,ExitDir,Cross,ExAP))
      Classifies one component. Separates value-1 and value-3 cells, calls
      arc2_sb_exit_dir_ to determine the exit direction, and computes the
      cross positions (cols for vertical beams, rows for horizontal beams)
      and exit axis position (row or col of the 3-markers).

  arc2_sb_exit_dir_(+Grid, +BG, +Cells3, -ExitDir)
      Determines the exit direction by the open-face method. The value-3
      cells are always collinear. If they share a row R3, check row R3-1
      (up) and row R3+1 (down) at the marker columns: the side that is all
      BG is the exit direction. If they share a column C3, check columns
      C3-1 (left) and C3+1 (right) at the marker rows similarly.

  arc2_sb_beam_(+Dir, +Cross, +AxisStart, +Shapes, +NR, +NC, +Acc, -Out)
      Recursive beam relay. Finds the closest shape in direction Dir that
      overlaps Cross positions. Fills the gap between AxisStart and the hit,
      adds the shape's cells, redirects the beam with the shape's exit
      parameters, and recurses. Base case fills the remaining gap to the
      grid boundary.

  arc2_sb_hit_(+Dir, +Cross, +AxisStart, +Shapes, -HitShape, -HitPos)
      Finds the shape closest to AxisStart in direction Dir whose cells
      include positions in Cross. Returns HitShape and HitPos (the row or
      column of the closest cell).

  arc2_sb_rect_(+Cross, +From, +To, +Mode, -Cells)
      Generates a rectangle of R-C positions. Mode v: rows vary from From
      to To, columns fixed to Cross list. Mode h: columns vary, rows fixed.

  arc2_sb_blank_(+NR, +NC, +BG, -Blank)
      Creates an NR x NC grid filled with BG.

KEY BUG AND FIX

Naive exit direction detection checks which value-1 cell is adjacent to a
value-3 cell. This fails at corner positions where a value-3 cell is adjacent
to value-1 cells in two directions simultaneously. The correct method: the
value-3 cells in every shape are always collinear (same row OR same column).
Check which adjacent row or column is all-background at the marker positions.
That is the open face, and it is the exit direction.
