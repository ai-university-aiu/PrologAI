Chapter 322: Wave 104 - box_tally (WP-362, Layer 337)

box_tally solves ARC-AGI-2 task 8f215267. The task gives three
training pairs and one test case, all grids 23 columns wide and
20 to 27 rows tall, and the output always keeps the input size.
Every input shows, along the left side, a stack of hollow
rectangular boxes - one-cell-thick frames five rows tall and
eleven columns wide, each in its own color, each with a pure
background interior - while the rest of the grid is sprinkled
with small scattered shapes of assorted colors: dominoes,
two-by-two squares, two-by-three blocks, plus signs, and
diamonds. In the output the scatter is gone: every box frame
stands exactly where it stood, its interior clean except for a
short row of dots of the box's own color printed on the middle
interior row, right-aligned starting two columns left of the
right frame edge and spaced two cells apart. Each box has
counted the separate scatter pieces of its own color, and
scatter colors owning no box simply vanish.

Rule: read the background as the most frequent color; split
every non-background color into 4-connected components; a
component that is exactly the border of its own bounding box of
at least three by three is a hollow box, everything else is
scatter; start from a blank background canvas, redraw every box
frame in place, and print one dot of the box color per scatter
component of that color on the middle interior row, at columns
right edge minus two, minus four, and so on.

Algorithm:

The transform first calls td_background_, which flattens the
grid, counts the palette with clumped, and takes the most
frequent color as the background. bt_fg_cells_ collects every
non-background cell as a position-color triple and bt_colors_
lists the distinct foreground colors. For each color,
bt_color_pts_ keeps that color's positions and bt_comps_ with
bt_grow_ groups them into 4-connected components by flood fill
over unit Manhattan neighbors. bt_hollow_box_ classifies a
component: bt_bbox_ computes its bounding box, the box must
span at least three rows and three columns, bt_border_
enumerates the sorted border positions of that bounding box,
and the component must equal the border exactly - a frame with
nothing missing and nothing extra. bt_color_boxes_ counts each
color's scatter components and pairs every box of that color
with the tally. Rendering starts from bt_blank_, a canvas of
pure background at the input size; bt_draw_entry_ paints each
box via bt_draw_frame_, which writes the border cells with
arc2_set_cell_, and bt_draw_dots_, which places the tally dots
on the middle interior row at columns right edge minus two
times one through the tally. The induce clause dispatches
early, directly after tower_donate, requiring equal first-pair
dimensions, at least two foreground colors, at least two hollow
boxes with pure background interiors, and pairwise distinct box
colors, then verifying every training pair.

The lesson of the wave is to distrust a prior wave's surface
reading. The task had been shelved for seven waves under a
stale note about instruction patches and stripe counts; a fresh
dump of the actual grids dissolved it in minutes. The
discriminating observation is that the tally counts components
rather than cells: the color-8 scatter of the first training
pair totals ten cells in four pieces of four different shapes
and the box prints exactly four dots, while the zero-scatter
color-2 box of the third training pair stays empty. Distractor
colors owning no box confirm the erase-everything baseline. The
rule passed the single-task test and the full regression on the
first compile.

Key predicates:
  bt_fg_cells_/3       - non-background cells with colors
  bt_comps_/2          - 4-connected components by flood fill
  bt_bbox_/2           - bounding box of a component
  bt_border_/2         - sorted border of a bounding box
  bt_hollow_box_/1     - component equals its bounding-box border
  bt_color_boxes_/3    - boxes paired with scatter tallies
  bt_blank_/4          - background canvas of the input size
  bt_draw_frame_/4     - repaint one box frame
  bt_draw_dots_/5      - right-aligned tally dots, spaced two
  arc2_transform(box_tally, +Grid, -Out)
  arc2_induce_rule(+Pairs, box_tally)
