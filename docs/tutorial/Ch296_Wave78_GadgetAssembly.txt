Chapter 296: Wave 78 - gadget_assembly (WP-336, Layer 311)

gadget_assembly solves ARC-AGI-2 task cbebaa4b. Each input grid (22x22
in training, 26x26 in the test) scatters five to seven gadget pieces
over the background. Each piece is an 8-connected component of one
body color carrying color-2 connector studs on its boundary. The task
is to slide the pieces together - translation only, no rotation - so
that every stud coincides exactly with its partner stud on another
piece, assembling all pieces into one connected machine on an output
canvas of the same size. The tell-tale signature: the color-2 cell
count halves from input to output (16 to 8, 16 to 8, and 24 to 12
across the three grids) because paired studs merge into one cell,
while every other color keeps its exact cell count.

Rule: studs pair by exact coincidence under translation with opposite
facings, and the connection graph's center piece keeps its exact
input position while the machine assembles around it.

Algorithm:

The background is the most common cell value. All non-background
cells are grouped into 8-connected components over mixed colors,
reusing the Wave 76 extraction engine unmodified. Each piece's
color-2 cells are its connector studs; a stud's outward facing is
read from its unique 4-adjacent non-stud cell of the same piece - the
stud points away from its body neighbor. Piece 1 seeds the assembly
at translation (0,0), and a backtracking search repeatedly takes the
first open stud and tries every stud of every unplaced piece as its
mate: the mate must face the opposite direction, and the whole piece
is translated by the delta that brings the two studs into exact
coincidence. A placement is accepted only if every colliding cell is
a stud landing on an open stud with opposite facing; each coincidence
closes both studs and records a connection edge. The search succeeds
when every piece is placed and no stud remains open. The recorded
edges form the machine's connection graph; breadth-first search gives
each piece's eccentricity, and the center piece - the one with
minimum eccentricity - is translated back to its exact input
position, carrying the whole assembly with it. The machine is painted
on a fresh background canvas of the input's dimensions. The induction
pre-filter requires equal input and output dimensions, an output
color-2 count exactly half the input's, and every other color count
preserved, then verifies all pairs with forall.

The lesson: the anchor is a graph-theoretic role. Wave 76 anchored on
a colored stud and Wave 77 on a role-bearing palette piece; Wave 78
anchors on the center of the connection graph, which in all three
grids is a simple path (5, 5, and 7 pieces long) with a unique
center. In this task the center always happens to be the color-4
piece, but the rule reads the center from the graph structure, not
from any color. A second lesson: committing to the first open stud is
complete, because coinciding studs pair at placement time, so an open
stud can never belong to two placed pieces - backtracking is needed
only over the mate choices.

Key predicates:
  ga_prep_/4           - piece occupancy cells and open studs with facings
  ga_dir_/4            - stud outward direction from its unique body neighbor
  ga_opp_/2            - two stud facings are compatible for pairing
  ga_loop_/8           - backtracking place-one-piece-per-open-stud search
  ga_merge_/8          - collision check that pairs coinciding studs
  ga_center_/3         - connection graph center by minimum eccentricity
  ga_render_/5         - assembled cells painted on a background canvas
  pf_comps_/2          - 8-connected mixed-color components (reused)
  arc2_transform(gadget_assembly, +Grid, -Out)

Task solved: cbebaa4b (2 training pairs + 1 test case).
