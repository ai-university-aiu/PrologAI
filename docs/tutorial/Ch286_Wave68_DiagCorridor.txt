Chapter 286: Wave 68 - diag_corridor (WP-326, Layer 301)

diag_corridor solves ARC-AGI-2 task 7666fa5d. Each 16x16 input grid
has background color 8 and one bank color per grid (4, 3, or 9). The
bank cells form slanted anti-diagonal chains - runs of cells stepping
(row+1, column-1) - that act as the two banks of a winding corridor.
The task is to flood every background cell lying between a pair of
opposing banks with color 2 (red), filling the corridor while leaving
the banks and all unbracketed background untouched.

Rule: the corridors look irregular in row-column space - a background
cell directly adjacent to a bank may stay empty while a farther cell
is filled - but they become plain rectangles after a change of
coordinates. With line sum s = r + c and cross coordinate d = r - c,
every anti-diagonal chain is an axis-aligned segment (constant s, an
interval in d), and the corridor between two banks is exactly the set
of cells strictly between the two chains in s whose d falls inside
the intersection of the two chains' d-intervals.

Algorithm:
(1) Background and banks: the background color is the most frequent
    cell value (dc_bg_/2); every other cell is a bank cell
    (dc_banks_/3).
(2) Chain extraction: bank cells are grouped into chains, the
    connected components under the anti-diagonal step (r+1, c-1)
    (dc_chains_/2, dc_grow_/5). Because the bank pool is sorted by
    row, the head of the pool is always the topmost cell of its own
    chain, so a single downward sweep with select/3 finds each chain
    without a visited set.
(3) Chain info: each chain is summarized by its shared line sum
    s = r + c and its cross interval [dmin, dmax] in d = r - c
    (dc_chain_info_/4).
(4) Corridor bracketing: for every unordered pair of chains with
    distinct line sums s1 < s2, a background cell belongs to their
    corridor when s1 < r + c < s2 and its cross coordinate lies inside
    [max(dmin1, dmin2), min(dmax1, dmax2)] (dc_fill_cell_/5).
(5) Flood: the union of all corridor regions over all chain pairs is
    deduplicated with sort/2 and painted with color 2 (dc_paint_/4);
    bank cells and unbracketed background cells are preserved.
(6) Induction pre-filter: equal input and output row counts, exactly
    two distinct input colors, and an output palette equal to the
    input palette plus color 2; then all training pairs are verified
    with forall.

Two subtleties make the rule honest. First, ALL pairs of chains
contribute corridors, not merely adjacent pairs: in training pair 2,
cells such as (8,13) are bracketed only by the outermost chain pair,
whose corridor covers regions no adjacent pair reaches. Second, two
distinct chains can share the same line sum - in training pair 1 two
separate chains both sit on s = 12 at opposite ends of the grid - so
chains must be separated by anti-diagonal connectivity, never merely
grouped by line sum, or phantom corridors appear.

The design insight is the coordinate transform: geometry that winds
in one frame is straight in another, and choosing the frame in which
the pattern is axis-aligned turns an irregular-looking fill into two
interval tests. No grid rotation is ever materialized; the transform
lives entirely in the arithmetic of the bracketing test.

Key predicates:
  dc_bg_/2          - most frequent cell value as background color
  dc_banks_/3       - sorted coordinates of all non-background cells
  dc_chains_/2      - anti-diagonal connected components of bank cells
  dc_grow_/5        - extend a chain by the next (r+1, c-1) neighbor
  dc_chain_info_/4  - line sum and cross range of a chain
  dc_fill_cell_/5   - background cell bracketed by a pair of chains
  dc_paint_/4       - repaint bracketed cells with color 2
  arc2_transform(diag_corridor, +Grid, -Out)
  arc2_induce_rule(+Pairs, diag_corridor)
