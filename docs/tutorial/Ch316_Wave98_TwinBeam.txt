Chapter 316: Wave 98 - twin_beam (WP-356, Layer 331)

twin_beam solves ARC-AGI-2 task 9bbf930d. The task gives three
training pairs and one test case. The grids are 17x16, 19x14,
and 17x16 in training and 23x21 in the test, and the output
always keeps the input size and changes only a handful of
cells. Every grid is a ladder: even rows carry a colored
horizontal bar starting near column two plus optional pipe
decorations to the right, odd rows are 7-filled separator
corridors, and every row opens with a 6 lamp in column zero on
a 7 spacer. In the first training pair the twin bars are 1s,
4s, and 9s; in the second 1s, 0s, and 3s; in the third 4s, 2s,
5s, and 3s; the test fires six separators among 9, 0, 1, 4, and
2 bars. Wherever two consecutive bars share one color, the
separator between them loses its lamp and a 6 appears somewhere
else in the grid - at the right end of the separator in the
clean second pair, but deep inside the pipe decorations, at the
top or bottom boundary, or beside a distant obstacle in the
others; one test beam crosses twenty rows and turns twice.

Rule: fire every separator whose neighbor bars share one color,
remove all fired lamps at once, and let each lamp travel as a
beam heading right through the 7 corridors - turning at an
obstacle onto its unique open side, refusing a left turn while
the current row still holds its own lamp, resting at boundaries
and two-way junctions, and painting a 6 where it rests.

Algorithm:

The transform first reads the ladder: tb_bar_color_ fetches a
bar row, skips the lamp and spacer columns, and hands the rest
to tb_first_fg_, which scans rightward for the first non-7
cell - so bars that start late, like the 9 bars of the first
pair, still read correctly. A findall collects every odd
separator row whose bar rows directly above and below share one
color, and at least one must fire. tb_douse_ then folds over
the fired rows, turning column zero of each from 6 to 7 on one
shared working grid before any beam is traced - the guard below
makes later beams consult this vacated state, so dousing lazily
per beam would make the outcome depend on trace order.
tb_trace_ walks one beam per fired row from column zero heading
right: while the next cell in the current heading holds 7 the
beam advances, and when the next cell steps off the grid the
beam rests on the cell it occupies. At an obstacle tb_turn_
takes over: it collects the two cells perpendicular to the
heading - up and down for a horizontal heading, left and right
for a vertical one - and tb_open_ keeps those that are inside
the grid and hold 7, on raw openness alone. With exactly one
open side the beam turns there and continues, unless
tb_guarded_ vetoes it: a turn onto heading left is refused
while column zero of the current row still shows a 6. With zero
or two open sides, or a refused left turn, the beam rests where
it stands. Finally tb_light_ folds over the resting cells and
paints a 6 on each, so no beam is blocked by another beam's
delivered lamp. The induce clause dispatches early, directly
after hub_dock, requiring equal first-pair dimensions and every
first-input row opening with a 6 lamp on a 7 spacer, then
verifying every training pair.

The lesson of the wave is that the lamp guard on left turns is
what separates two junctions no local rule can tell apart. In
the third training pair a beam descends a pipe and rests beside
an open leftward corridor because that row still holds its 6
lamp; in the test grid a beam turns left along a fired
separator whose lamp has already departed - and the two
junctions are identical in every cell of their neighborhoods.
The vacated column-zero lamps are not cosmetic; they are state
that later beams consult, which is why all fired lamps leave
before any beam walks. A second lesson is that junction arity
is tested on raw openness before the guard is applied: the
third pair's final beam faces a two-way junction and must rest
there, even though the guard would have thinned its choice to a
single side - the first Python prototype applied the guard too
early and failed exactly that pair.

Key predicates:

  tb_light_/3          - paint a 6 on one resting cell
  tb_bar_color_/3      - bar color: first non-7 from column two
  tb_first_fg_/2       - scan a row tail for the first non-7
  tb_trace_/9          - walk one beam to its resting cell
  tb_turn_/9           - resolve a block: turn or rest
  tb_open_/6           - side cell inside the grid holding 7
  tb_guarded_/3        - left turn vetoed by the row's own lamp
