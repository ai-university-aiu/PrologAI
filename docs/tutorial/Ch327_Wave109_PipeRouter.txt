Chapter 327: Wave 109 - pipe_router (WP-367, Layer 342)

pipe_router solves ARC-AGI-2 task e87109e9. The task gives three
training pairs and one test case, on inputs of 25x24 and 30x24
with outputs of 19x24 and 24x24. Every input is a two-part scene:
a six-row legend strip of framed color swatches across the top,
and a playing field below holding a majority-color background,
colored rectangular blocks, and one square seed of 8s. The
background is 3, 1, 7, and 9 across the four grids, so every role
is read off each grid rather than memorized. The output is the
field alone, legend dropped, with a network of color-8 pipes
painted onto it.

Rule: split off the six legend rows; decode each six-wide swatch
from the second legend row - the colored interior pixel (neither
0 nor frame color 5) names a block color, and the side of the
swatch the pixel occupies is a one-bit instruction, left half
meaning blocks of that color turn pipes LEFT and right half
meaning they turn pipes RIGHT; read the majority color of the
field as the background and bound the square seed of 8s, whose
side length is the pipe width; shoot four pipes from the seed,
one per orthogonal direction, each as wide as the seed; march
each pipe one front line at a time, painting background cells 8
and treating pipe cells and the seed as passable, ending the pipe
when its front line leaves the grid; and when a front line
contains any block cell, stop beside the block, paint the head
square as a corner, turn left or right by the blocking color's
legend handedness, and march on from the corner.

The delicate part is the turn direction. Sixteen block-hit turns
occur across the four grids, and they follow no fixed chirality,
no larger-extent rule, no nearest-edge rule, and no path-order
story - single-snake readings, legend-order chains, torus wraps,
and spatial clockwise cycles all fail on some grid. Cataloguing
all sixteen turns as independent events and asking what per-color
property predicts them gave the answer: the swatch pixel side
matches sixteen of sixteen, so the legend is a per-color
instruction table and the left-to-right order of the swatches is
irrelevant.

Two generalizations matter. The painted network branches from one
point in all four directions, so it is modeled as four
independent pipes leaving a seed rather than one continuous path.
And the training seeds are 2x2 while the test seed is 3x3, so the
stroke width is derived from the seed bounding box rather than
hard-coded. The induce clause dispatches early, directly after
cavity_paint, requiring rows one and six of the first input
entirely frame color 5 and the first output exactly six rows
shorter than the first input at equal width, then verifying every
training pair exactly. The complete rule was written in plain
English and verified cell by cell against every grid before any
Prolog, and the integrated rule passed the single-task test and
the full regression on the first compile.
