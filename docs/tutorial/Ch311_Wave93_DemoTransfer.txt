Chapter 311: Wave 93 - demo_transfer (WP-351, Layer 326)

demo_transfer solves ARC-AGI-2 task 4c7dc4dd. The task gives two
training pairs and one test case. All three inputs are 30x30
scenes filled with a noisy diagonal color pattern, and the outputs
are 5x5, 4x4, and 5x5 - the output size follows the ring boxes
found in the scene. Every input holds four square ring boxes:
complete one-color rectangle outlines sharing one frame color and
one side length, each interior resting on a zero background with
sparse colored marks. One interior is entirely empty and marks the
answer slot. Two interiors share the same set of nonzero colors
and form a worked demonstration - the before and the after of a
transformation. The fourth interior, carrying its own distinct
colors, is the question. The output is the demonstrated
transformation applied to the question interior.

Rule: the grid itself teaches the transformation. The first
training scene demonstrates connect (markers joined by straight
filled segments), the second demonstrates complement (zero and
the mark color swap places), and the test scene demonstrates
ripple (a radial pattern around the center moves outward and
repeats) - an operation that appears in no training output, so
the transformation must be read off the scene at answer time.

Algorithm:

The transform first scans the scene with dt_ring_ for complete
square rings of one nonzero color with side five to nine whose
interiors hold at most two distinct nonzero colors and at least
four zero cells. dt_boxes_ groups the candidate rings by frame
color and side length and keeps the group holding exactly four
rings, slicing out their interiors with dt_interior_.
dt_classify_ then sets aside the all-zero interior as the answer
slot, pairs the two interiors sharing one nonzero color set as
the demonstration, and takes the remaining interior as the
question - color grouping alone separates demo from question, so
the noisy region frames of the scene never need to be parsed.
dt_learn_ checks a library of three operations against the
demonstration pair in both directions through dt_op_ and
dt_apply_: complement swaps zero with the single mark color;
connect fills every straight run of zeros between two collinear
marks with the non-corner mark color, trying each mark color as
the corner and scanning columns by transposing the grid;
ripple reads a Manhattan-distance color profile around the center
of the odd-sided square with dt_ring_val_, requires leading mark
rings followed by zero rings, and pushes the pattern outward by
its own extent, repeating it periodically with dt_ripple_val_.
When both directions match - complement is its own inverse -
dt_learn_ prefers the direction whose source box has a
nonzero-cell count closest to the question box. Finally
dt_apply_ runs the learned operation on the question interior
with the question's own colors: the corner color learned from
the demonstration persists, and the fill color is the question's
other mark. The induce clause dispatches early, directly after
stencil_carve, requiring a first input of height at least twenty,
a first output no larger than eight by eight, and four ring boxes
in the first input, then verifying every training pair.

The lesson of the wave is the in-grid worked example. Any solver
that memorizes what the training pairs did fails the test by
construction, because the test demands an operation no training
output contains; the rule must understand the demonstration
inside each scene and transfer it to new colors. A second lesson
is that direction must be earned: position-based pairing of demo
and question boxes gives the wrong direction on the test grid,
while matching the question's nonzero-cell count against each
demonstration box picks the correct source in all three scenes.

Key predicates:
  dt_boxes_/2          - group rings and slice the four interiors
  dt_ring_/7           - find one complete square ring candidate
  dt_interior_/5       - cut a ring interior out of the scene
  dt_classify_/4       - split answer slot, demo pair, question
  dt_learn_/4          - pick the operation and its direction
  dt_op_/3             - check one library operation on the demo
  dt_apply_/3          - run complement, connect, or ripple
  dt_ring_val_/4       - read one radial profile color
  arc2_transform(demo_transfer, +Grid, -Out)
  arc2_induce_rule(+Pairs, demo_transfer)
