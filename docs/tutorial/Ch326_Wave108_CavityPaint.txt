Chapter 326: Wave 108 - cavity_paint (WP-366, Layer 341)

cavity_paint solves ARC-AGI-2 task 3a25b0d8. The task gives two
training pairs and one test case, on inputs of 22x22 and 30x30
with outputs of 11x11, 13x7, and 18x13. Every input holds exactly
two foreground shapes on a uniform majority-color background, and
the two shapes are the same figure drawn twice. One drawing is a
hollow silhouette: a closed skeleton drawn entirely in a single
color whose enclosed background pockets are the figure's
cavities. The other is a multi-color template: the same figure
redrawn at a different size and visibly warped, with its cavities
already filled with paint. The background is 8, 1, and 0 and the
silhouette color is 1, 2, and 4 across the three grids, so every
role is read off each grid rather than memorized.

Rule: read the majority color as the background; flood the
foreground into 8-connected components and require exactly two -
the component drawn in a single color is the mask and the
component drawn in two or more colors is the template; crop the
output to the mask's bounding box and keep every mask cell in the
mask color; flood the in-box background 4-connectedly from the
border cells, so the unreached background cells are the enclosed
cavities, grouped into 4-connected regions; collect the
template's paint regions - template cells whose color is neither
the background nor the mask color, grouped per color into
4-connected regions; then paint each cavity with the color of the
paint region whose centroid, normalized to its own shape's
bounding box, lies nearest to the cavity's normalized centroid in
squared euclidean distance, and leave the open background as
background.

The delicate part is the pairing. The template warp shifts
regions off their rows, their columns, and even their
top-to-bottom ranks: in the first training pair the silhouette's
middle cavity starts one row below its flanking cavities while
its template twin starts level with its own flankers, so ordinal
matching, absolute position, and region size all assign the wrong
color. The invariant that survives the warp is position
proportional to the bounding box - each region's normalized
centroid lands nearly on top of its counterpart's, and the
nearest-centroid match lands every color on every cavity across
both training pairs and all nine cavities of the test.

Connectivity arity matters twice. The shapes are split
8-connectedly so diagonal joints hold a figure together, while
cavities and paint regions are split 4-connectedly so a diagonal
gap neither leaks a cavity into the open background nor welds two
paint regions into one. Normalization divides by each bounding
box extent with a floor of one, so a one-row or one-column shape
cannot divide by zero. The induce clause dispatches early,
directly after ray_merge, requiring the first output strictly
smaller than the first input in both dimensions and a successful
mono-plus-multi split, then verifying every training pair
exactly. The complete rule was written in plain English and
verified cell by cell against every grid before any Prolog, and
the integrated rule passed the single-task test and the full
regression on the first compile.
