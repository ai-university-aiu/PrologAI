Chapter 349: Theory of Mind - The tom Pack (tm_*, Layer 364)

The final pack of the AGI Foundations suite tackles the social pillar of
general intelligence: modelling other minds. The ability being modelled
has a famous test, and this chapter is built around it.

THE SALLY-ANNE TEST

Sally puts a marble in her basket while Anne watches. Sally leaves the
room. Anne moves the marble into her box. Sally comes back. Where will
Sally look for her marble?

Children under four say "the box" - they know where the marble IS, and
cannot yet separate that from where Sally BELIEVES it is. Passing the test
means holding two versions of the world at once: reality, and someone
else's outdated picture of it.

Here is the whole scenario:

    ?- tm_new(M0),
       tm_event(M0, loc(marble, basket), [sally, anne], M1),
       tm_event(M1, loc(marble, box),    [anne],        M2).

tm_event is where the magic lives. When a fact changes with witnesses,
three things happen: the world is updated; every witness revises their own
belief; and every witness records that the other witnesses saw it too.
Absent agents keep their old beliefs. False beliefs are not programmed in -
they are what happens to whoever was out of the room.

Now the questions:

    ?- tm_belief(M2, sally, loc(marble, Where)).
    Where = basket.                       % Sally will look in the basket

    ?- tm_knows(M2, anne, loc(marble, box)).
    true.                                 % Anne knows - true belief

    ?- tm_knows(M2, sally, loc(marble, basket)).
    false.                                % Sally believes, but does not know

    ?- tm_false_beliefs(M2, sally, F).
    F = [loc(marble, basket)].            % exactly her stale belief

THE SECOND-ORDER QUESTION

The harder test: where does ANNE think SALLY will look? Answering needs a
belief about a belief.

    ?- tm_attribute(M2, anne, sally, Beliefs).
    Beliefs = [loc(marble, basket)].

Anne watched Sally watch the first event, so Anne models Sally's basket
belief. Anne alone witnessed the move, so her model of Sally never
updated. The nested bookkeeping came free from the witness mechanics.

MORE INSTRUMENTS

tm_divergent lists exactly where two agents contradict each other.
tm_common lists what everyone in a group shares - the marble's location is
common belief before the move and common to no one after it.
tm_perspective re-seats the whole model inside one agent's head: Sally's
beliefs become the world, and her beliefs about others drop one level, so
you can reason AS Sally using every other predicate unchanged.

And because agents want things and commit to things, tm_desire and
tm_intend record per-agent goals and intentions - the belief-desire-
intention triple that social prediction is built from.

The mental model is a pure term: every update returns a new model, so
imagining "what would Anne believe if..." can never corrupt the record of
what anyone actually believes.

All 20 acceptance tests pass, eleven of them the Sally-Anne scenario
itself, from first-order search prediction to second-order attribution.

This closes the AGI Foundations suite: causal, actinf, worldmodel,
planner, evolve, jspace, and tom - seven packs, 120 public predicates,
154 tests, all green.
