Chapter 247: Wave 29 - diag_beam (WP-287, Layer 262)

Wave 29 adds the diag_beam named rule to arc_benchmark_2.pl, solving
ARC-AGI-2 task db695cfb. This rule recognizes that 1-cells placed exactly
45 degrees apart from each other define diagonal beams, and that 6-cells
on a beam path act as reflectors that send perpendicular 6-trails outward.

WHAT THE RULE DOES

The grid contains only three types of cells: background, 1-cells, and
6-cells. Two 1-cells are diagonally aligned when |row_diff| = |col_diff| > 0.
Each aligned pair defines a 45-degree beam. The beam fills intermediate
background cells with 1. When the beam hits a 6-cell, it leaves the 6
unchanged and fires two perpendicular 45-degree rays from that point. Each
ray fills background cells with 6 until it hits the grid boundary or
another existing 6-cell.

KEY PREDICATES

  arc2_transform(diag_beam, Grid, Out)
      Top-level guard: reads background from Grid[0][0], verifies all
      non-background cells are 1 or 6, collects Ones and Sixes, finds
      all diagonal 1-pairs, and applies each beam via foldl.

  arc2_db_march_(R, C, DR, DC, R2, C2, Sixes, NRows, NCols, G0, G1)
      Base clause stops at the destination (R2, C2). Recursive clause
      checks memberchk(R-C, Sixes): if true, bounces; otherwise fills
      the cell with 1 and steps to (R+DR, C+DC).

  arc2_db_bounce_(R, C, DR, DC, Sixes, NRows, NCols, G0, G1)
      Fires two perpendicular rays: directions (DR, -DC) and (-DR, DC).

  arc2_db_ray_(R, C, DR, DC, Sixes, NRows, NCols, G0, G1)
      Extends the ray one step at a time. Stops at boundary or existing 6.
      Fills background cells with 6 and recurses.
