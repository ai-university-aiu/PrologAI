Chapter 333: Wave 115 - pinwheel_spiral (WP-373, Layer 348)

Wave 115 adds a new glass-box rule, pinwheel_spiral, that solves
ARC-AGI-2 task da515329. The score rises from 111/120 = 92.50% to
112/120 = 93.33%.

This task is a lesson in how a large, complex figure can be the
deterministic unfolding of one tiny seed. The input shows almost
nothing: a single small plus sign (a cross) of one color, with an
empty center, floating on an otherwise blank background. The
output keeps the grid size and the two colors but fills the ENTIRE
grid with a dense interlocking spiral in the seed color.

READING THE PLUS AS A MACHINE

Think of the plus as a pinwheel hub. Its center is empty and it
has four equal arms. Each arm is the starting tail of a square
spiral that winds outward, and every spiral turns the SAME way -
clockwise. Because the four spirals are ninety-degree rotations of
one another, their walls mesh together like the teeth of four
gears as they wind out, leaving exactly a one-cell gap everywhere.
That meshed pattern is a period-two pinwheel: it tiles the whole
board with a spiral whose lines are one cell apart.

THE ONE HIDDEN NUMBER

Everything is controlled by a single parameter: the arm length L,
the number of cells from the center to a tip. Three lengths follow
from it:

  the outward run   fr    = max(1, L - 1)
  the sideways jog  jog   = max(0, L - 2)
  the trailing run  small = max(2, L)

Each arm is a turtle. It starts at the seed cell one step out from
the center, walks fr cells outward to the tip, and turns ninety
degrees clockwise. Then it repeats layers k = 0, 1, 2, ...:

  walk a leading run of 2 + 4k cells (2, 6, 10, 14, ...),
  jog max(0, L - 2) cells sideways in the clockwise direction,
  walk a trailing run of max(2, L) cells,
  turn ninety degrees clockwise.

Cells that leave the grid are simply not painted. Four turtles,
one per arm, paint the whole pinwheel.

WHY THE EASY EXAMPLES HIDE THE LAW

The jog max(0, L - 2) is zero when L is 1 or 2. So the two smaller
training pairs have perfectly straight spiral sides and look like
an ordinary interlocking spiral - a naive first model of runs
1, 4, 8, 12, ... reproduces them exactly and hides the real law.
Only the L = 3 training pair reveals the truth: its spiral sides
are STEPPED, carrying a one-cell jog and a trailing run that grows
with L. The unified turtle is the only law that reproduces all
three training pairs AND the held-out test, whose plus has arm
length L = 4 and whose spiral sides jog by two. This is the
discriminating observation of the wave.
