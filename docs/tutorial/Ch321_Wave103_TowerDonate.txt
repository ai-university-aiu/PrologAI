Chapter 321: Wave 103 - tower_donate (WP-361, Layer 336)

tower_donate solves ARC-AGI-2 task 9aaea919. The task gives
three training pairs and one test case, all on 30x30 grids, and
the output always keeps the input size. Every input shows
towers of diamond glyphs standing on a uniform background. A
glyph is a three-row, five-column stamp: the middle row is
solid in the glyph color, and the top and bottom rows carry the
color over the middle three cells with background corners. The
glyphs stack upward from the bottom of the grid, one background
row between glyphs, so consecutive centers sit four rows apart,
and every glyph of a tower shares one color. The bottom grid
row carries five-wide marker bars aligned exactly under
specific towers: bars of color 2 and bars of color 3, with the
remaining towers unmarked. In the output the markers have done
their work and vanished: every tower under a color-2 bar keeps
its height but is repainted gray with color 5, every tower
under a color-3 bar has grown taller in its own color, and the
unmarked towers are untouched.

Rule: read the background as the most frequent color; scan the
bottom row into five-wide bars of colors 2 (donor) and 3
(receiver); detect the diamond glyphs and group them into
single-color towers by column window; sum the donor tower
heights into the donated total; repaint every donor tower gray
5 at its own height; grow every receiver tower by the donated
total, stacking new glyphs on top four rows apart; erase the
bars.

Algorithm:

The transform first calls td_background_, which flattens the
grid, counts the palette with clumped, and takes the most
frequent color as the background. td_bars_ with td_row_runs_
and td_take_run_ scans the bottom grid row left to right,
skipping background cells and reading each non-background run;
every run must be exactly five cells wide and colored 2 or 3,
and each becomes a bar term holding its color and start column.
td_glyphs_ enumerates every candidate center row and window
start column, and td_glyph_ tests the full thirteen-cell
diamond pattern - a solid five-wide middle row, three-wide top
and bottom arcs over the middle columns, and background corners
- collecting each hit with its center row, start column, and
color. td_towers_ groups the glyphs by their five-wide column
window, requires every glyph of a group to share one color, and
sorts the center rows top to bottom; every bar must sit exactly
under a tower's window. The donation is then plain arithmetic:
the glyph counts of all towers under color-2 bars sum into the
donated total. td_gray_tower_ walks each donor's centers and
td_paint_glyph_ repaints its thirteen cells with color 5, so
the donor keeps its exact height and silhouette. td_grow_tower_
takes each receiver tower's topmost center row and paints the
donated number of new glyphs in the tower's own color, each
center four rows above the previous one. Finally td_erase_bar_
with td_set_bg_ wipes the five cells of every marker bar back
to the background. The induce clause dispatches early, directly
after ray_scatter, requiring equal first-pair dimensions, a
bottom row holding only five-wide bars of colors 2 and 3 with
at least one of each, at least one glyph, and every bar aligned
under a tower, then verifying every training pair.

The lesson of the wave is to look for a conserved quantity
flowing between marked columns. The three training receivers
grow by two, one, and three glyphs while the three donors stand
two, one, and three glyphs tall - a match that would also fit a
rule reading the donor as a mere gauge. The test grid settles
it: two donor bars over two one-glyph towers grow the single
receiver by exactly two glyphs, so the donations must be summed
across all donors. Reading all four grids before theorizing
made the arithmetic obvious in one pass, and the rule passed
the single-task test and the full regression on the first
compile.
