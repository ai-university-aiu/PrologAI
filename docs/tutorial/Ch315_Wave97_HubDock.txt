Chapter 315: Wave 97 - hub_dock (WP-355, Layer 330)

hub_dock solves ARC-AGI-2 task 2c181942. The task gives three
training pairs and one test case. The grids are 23x20, 18x21,
and 20x25 in training and 24x26 in the test, and the output
always keeps the input size. Every scene shows one hub piece -
an eight-cell ring in a four-by-four bounding box whose four
sides carry two centered cells each, every side in its own
color - plus loose single-color shapes scattered on the
background. In the first training pair the hub sides are 6 on
top, 3 on the left, 2 on the right, and 1 on the bottom, and
the loose shapes are 2s, 3s, and 4s; in the third the sides are
1, 2, 7, and 3, and the shapes are 1s, 2s, 3s, and 7s; in the
test the sides are 1, 4, 2, and 6, and the shapes are 1s, 2s,
3s, 4s, and 6s. The output rebuilds the scene from a fresh
background canvas: the hub stays exactly where it was, every
shape whose color matches a hub side is rotated and parked
flush against that side, and every shape whose color matches no
side vanishes - the 4 shape in the first pair and the 3 shape
in the test.

Rule: find the hub as the only eight-connected component mixing
four colors, read its four sides as two adjacent same-colored
cells each, and dock every matching shape by the unique quarter
turn that puts a bounding-box side holding exactly two adjacent
centered cells toward its hub side, parked flush so the two
contact cells sit directly next to the two hub edge cells.

Algorithm:

The transform first takes the most common color as the
background with arc2_bg_color_ and groups the foreground cells
into eight-connected components with the Wave 82 helper
tw_components_. hd_hub_ keeps the components mixing exactly
four distinct colors, and exactly one may exist: the hub, an
eight-cell ring in a four-by-four bounding box. hd_edge_ reads
each side of the box - top row, bottom row, left column, right
column - as exactly two adjacent cells of one color and records
the color, the direction, and the start of the pair; the four
side colors must all differ. arc2_sb_blank_ builds an
all-background canvas of the input size and hd_put_ paints the
hub back unchanged. hd_dock_ then walks every other component:
the shape must be single-colored, and its color is looked up
among the sides - a shape owning no side is dropped. For a
matching shape, hd_orient_ normalizes the cells to the
bounding-box corner and tries the four quarter-turn rotations
in order through hd_rot_, which maps (R, C) to (C, H-1-R) per
turn; hd_face_ collects the side line facing the hub and
hd_centered_ accepts it only when the side length is even and
the line holds exactly the two centered positions. hd_place_
computes the corner that parks the rotated bounding box flush
against the hub side - one cell above, below, left, or right -
with the first contact cell aligned to the edge start, and
scc_paint_ writes the docked cells after a bounds check. The
induce clause dispatches early, directly after junction_hub,
requiring equal first-pair dimensions and five to eight
distinct colors in the first input, then verifying every
training pair.

The lesson of the wave is that centered contact is what makes
the docking unique. The second training pair's left shape also
owns a three-cell side whose two cells would touch the hub edge
rows, but only its bottom side holds exactly the two centered
positions, so a plain adjacency check would accept an off-center
fit that the centered-contact condition rejects - and the same
condition throws out the full-length sides too, pinning both
the rotation and the translation uniquely. A second lesson is
that the rotations are pure quarter turns with no reflection,
which the first training pair proves in both directions: the
same shape family docks left by a clockwise turn and right by a
counterclockwise turn. A third lesson extends Waves 95 and 96:
the side colors permute freely across pairs, so each side's
role is derived from its own two edge cells at answer time,
never memorized from color identities.

Key predicates:
  hd_hub_/1            - component mixing exactly four colors
  hd_edge_/5           - one hub side: two adjacent same cells
  hd_put_/3            - write one colored cell onto the grid
  hd_dock_/7           - dock or drop one loose shape
  hd_orient_/7         - first quarter turn with centered contact
  hd_rot_/7            - rotate cells K quarter turns clockwise
  hd_face_/4           - side line facing the hub
  hd_centered_/2       - exactly the two centered positions
  hd_place_/7          - flush corner against the hub side
  arc2_transform(hub_dock, +Grid, -Out)
  arc2_induce_rule(+Pairs, hub_dock)
