Chapter 330: Wave 112 - anchor_jigsaw (WP-370, Layer 345)

anchor_jigsaw solves ARC-AGI-2 task 446ef5d2. The task gives two
training pairs (grids 12x20 and 10x10 on background 8) and one
test case (a 26x26 grid on background 4). Every input scatters
ragged colored FRAGMENTS across the grid - four in each training
input, ten in the test input, two of them non-rectangular - plus
one three-cell L-TROMINO MARKER drawn in a color that appears
nowhere else (4 in training, 2 in the test).

The marker is a corner bracket packing three commands into three
cells. Its cells fill a two-by-two bounding box except for one
missing cell, the CUPPED CELL P. The diagonal step from the
marker's corner cell to P names which corner of the finished
picture sits exactly on P - bottom-right in train 1, top-left in
train 2, bottom-left in the test. The fragments are jigsaw
pieces: they tile, by translation alone, a single rectangle whose
area equals their total cell count, the piece containing P is
pinned at that corner and keeps its absolute grid position, and
the rectangle's outer ring is uniformly the border color - the
fragment color found at P.

Rule: find the background as the most common color and the marker
as the color occupying exactly three cells in a two-by-two box
minus one; take every 4-connected non-background, non-marker
component as a piece normalized to its bounding-box origin; for
every height-width factorization of the total cell count that
fits the tallest and widest piece, pin the anchor piece at the
P-corner and tile the rest by backtracking - the piece whose
row-major first cell covers the first empty cell goes next, with
no overlaps, no out-of-bounds cells, and the border color forced
on the outer ring; keep only coherent assemblies and paint the
first onto a blank background grid with its named corner on P.

The delicate part is the COHERENCE LAW. An exact-cover tiler
armed only with the uniform-border constraint finds TWO complete
tilings of the 22x10 test rectangle - and the false one first,
with its color-3 and color-7 frames each torn into two separated
clusters. The fix is the human jigsaw instinct made formal: every
non-border color of the finished picture must form exactly one
4-connected component. That single demand rejects the incoherent
assembly, accepts the true stacked nested-ring picture, and holds
silently on both training pairs.

One verification detail matters. Eyeballing the 26-column test
dump misplaced the assembled rectangle by one row and falsely
broke the anchor law; the programmatic diff of the fresh dumps
restored it before any code was written. The induce clause
dispatches early, directly after fractal_stamp, requiring equal
input and output dimensions and the unique-color L-tromino
marker on the first pair, then verifying every training pair
exactly. The integrated rule passed the single-task test and the
full regression on the first compile.
