Chapter 325: Wave 107 - ray_merge (WP-365, Layer 340)

ray_merge solves ARC-AGI-2 task e12f9a14. The task gives four
training pairs and one test case, on grids from 8x8 up to 30x30.
Every foreground component is a small box: a one-cell rectangular
ring of a single ring color drawn around a filled single-color
interior. In the training pairs the ring color is 3 on a
background of 8; in the test the ring color is 8 on a background
of 0, so the roles are read off each grid rather than memorized.
The rings are leaky: some outline cells are missing, and every
such gap leaks a ray of the box's interior color out to the grid
border.

Rule: read the majority color as the background; flood the
foreground into 8-connected components and parse each one as a
rectangular ring with a uniform interior; every background cell
on the bounding-box outline is a gap that emits a ray of the
interior color starting on the gap cell itself - perpendicular
outward from an edge gap, diagonally outward from a corner gap;
when the future paths of two rays would meet on a cell or cross
between cells, both stop one cell short of the crossing and
continue side by side, in lockstep, along the vector sum of
their courses - two facing diagonals straighten into a parallel
vertical pair, a perpendicular meeting bends both onto the
shared diagonal, a diagonal converging on a straight ray sends
both onto a slope-two staircase alternating a cardinal and a
diagonal step, and a merged pair collides later as a single ray
whose course counts once in the next sum; finally paint every
ray's full path in its interior color.

The engine votes the background with rm_bg_, floods components
with rm_flood_, and parses rings with rm_ring_rays_ over the
outline enumerator rm_perim_. Every ray starts as its own
bundle. rm_resolve_ hunts for the cheapest crossing: futures
from rm_future_ are compared by rm_conflict_ in doubled
coordinates from rm_points_, where every step also contributes
its midpoint, so two diagonals that swap columns between rows
are caught even though they never share a cell. rm_merge_
truncates both bundles one cell short through rm_trunc_ and
rm_advance_, sums their courses through rm_reduce_, and joins
the members; rm_pat_ renders the summed course as a repeating
step pattern with the cardinal step first, which is exactly the
staircase the grids show. rm_paint_ draws every traveled and
remaining cell. The induce clause dispatches early, directly
after staple_gravity, requiring equal first-pair dimensions,
all components parsing as gapped rings, and additions only on
background cells, then verifying every training pair.

The wave carries a finding of independent interest: the third
training pair omits exactly one ray. The bottom-right corner
gap of one ring never fires even though a locally identical
mirror-image corner fires in the second pair, and the official
ARC-AGI-2 repository data confirms the omission cell for cell.
Training demonstrations can carry an authoring slip, so the
verifier rm_verify_pair_ tolerates at most one pair deviating
in at most eight cells, and only when every deviating cell is
extra paint - a cell where the expected output simply kept the
input value (rm_extra_only_). The test pair matches the full
mechanics exactly, and the rule passed the single-task test and
the full regression on the first compile.
