Chapter 310: Wave 92 - stencil_carve (WP-350, Layer 325)

stencil_carve solves ARC-AGI-2 task 58f5dbd5. The task gives three
training pairs and one test case. The inputs are 19x19, 19x19,
23x18, and 23x18, and the outputs are 19x7, 7x19, 13x13, and
19x13 - the output shape is not fixed but follows the scene. Every
input holds several solid five-by-five monochrome square blocks
standing in a regular grid arrangement with one-cell background
gaps - one column of three blocks, one row of three blocks, a
two-by-two arrangement, and a three-by-two arrangement across the
four grids - and each block carries a distinct non-background
color. Elsewhere in the scene lie small three-by-three sketch
motifs of various colors, including decoy sketches whose colors
have no matching block. The output keeps the block arrangement on
a fresh background canvas and carves each block's central
three-by-three region with the negative of its same-colored
sketch: wherever the sketch shows the block color the output cell
turns background, and wherever the sketch shows background the
cell keeps the block color.

Rule: each solid block pairs with the detached sketch of its own
color, and the sketch acts as a stencil whose colored cells are
the holes - the block interior is the sketch's punched-out
negative, not its copy.

Algorithm:

The transform first takes the background as the most common color
with tw_background_. stc_block_ then scans for solid five-by-five
squares of one non-background color, anchoring each block at a
top-left corner whose upper and left neighbors differ from the
block color, so a larger same-colored region can never yield
duplicate placements and no overlap bookkeeping is needed. The
distinct block starting rows and starting columns are sorted into
the arrangement axes, and the block count must equal the product
of the row count and the column count, so the blocks fill a
complete row-by-column arrangement. For each block color,
stc_sketch_ collects every cell of that color outside all blocks -
stc_in_block_ supplies the membership test - and requires the
bounding box of the collection to be exactly three by three,
recording the offsets of the colored cells inside the box. The
sketches may be disconnected within their bounding boxes, so
collecting all same-colored cells beats connected-component
analysis, and decoy colors without a block are simply never
visited. stc_tile_ carves each block's five-by-five tile with
stc_tcell_: central cells at sketch offsets turn background, and
every other tile cell keeps the block color. Finally the output
canvas - height six times the block rows plus one, width six times
the block columns plus one - is assembled cell by cell with
stc_cell_, which uses six-cell band arithmetic (row and column
modulo six) to decide whether a cell falls inside a block tile and
reads the tile or paints background accordingly. The induce clause
dispatches early, directly after catalog_query, requiring the
first output's height and width to both be one more than a
multiple of six with a background top-left corner, then verifying
every training pair.

The lesson of the wave is the carving inversion. The naive reading
copies the sketch into the block interior and fails every pair;
the correct reading punches the sketch out of the solid block, so
a sketch cell of the block color becomes a background hole. A
second lesson is that the output dimensions are derived, not
assumed: the four grids exercise four different block
arrangements, and the canvas resizes to six cells per block row or
column plus one border cell. The test grid presents the largest
arrangement - three block rows by two block columns of colors 4,
8, 2, 1, 6, and 9 - carved from six sketches scattered in the left
third of the scene.
